# ⚔️ Valheim Boss Strategies Guide

*Last updated: June 2026 | Game version: 0.219.14+*

---

There are seven **Forsaken bosses** in Valheim, one per major biome, and each demands radically different preparation. This guide gives you the exact spawn items, arena setup, gear recommendations, food loadout, and fight tactics for every boss from **Eikthyr** to **Fader**.

---

## 🌲 Eikthyr — Meadows

The first boss and your introduction to Valheim's combat. Eikthyr is a giant stag with two attack types — a forward headbutt and a lightning bolt that arcs along the ground.

### Spawn & Summon

| Item | How to Get |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **2 Deer Trophy** | Hunt deer with a bow — headshots have ~50% trophy drop rate |
| **1 Greydwarf Eye** | Kill greydwarves in the Black Forest at night |

Take these to **Eikthyr's Altar** — a large stone ring with runes, marked on your map by a red icon after you find it. Interact with the altar to summon.

### Recommended Gear

- **Weapon:** Flint axe or crude bow (fire arrows optional but helpful)
- **Armor:** Leather tunic / rags (armor barely matters here)
- **Food:** 3x cooked meat, 3x raspberry, 3x mushroom
- **Optional:** 1x campfire (rested bonus only)

### Arena Setup

Clear the area around the altar of trees and rocks — you need a wide, flat circle to dodge. Place a **campfire** 20m away for the rested bonus. No need for portals; the meadows are safe.

### Fight Strategy

Eikthyr has two phases, triggered by health thresholds (~70% HP).

**Phase 1 (100%–70% HP):**
- Stays mostly stationary. Sprint sideways to dodge his headbutt.
- Charged headbutt: roll **just before** impact. The hitbox is narrow.
- Lightning bolt: a thin line on the ground — step sideways. Takes 2 seconds to charge.

**Phase 2 (70%–0% HP):**
- Gains a **ground slam** — AOE lightning in a 6m radius. Back away when he rises.
- More frequent lightning bolts. Keep moving.
- He summons **no adds** — it's a pure 1v1.

**Kill strategy:** Use the crude bow with fire arrows and kite him around the altar. 60–80 arrows will do the job. If you prefer melee, wait for the headbutt, roll sideways, and land 2–3 hits on his flank.

**Drop:** Hard Antler (crafts the **Antler Pickaxe** — your first pick). **Power:** +60% Run Speed for 3 minutes.

## 🦌 The Elder — Black Forest

A massive walking tree ent. The Elder is the first real gear check — don't fight him with leather gear.

### Spawn & Summon

| Item | How to Get |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **3 Ancient Seed** | Found in burial chambers (yellow glowing orbs) or dropped by Greydwarf Brutes/Shamans |

The Elder's altar is a large stone platform in the Black Forest surrounded by ancient trees.

### Recommended Gear

- **Weapon:** Finewood bow + 200 fire arrows (primary). Bronze sword/mace as backup.
- **Armor:** Full bronze armor set (or troll hide if you're confident dodging)
- **Food:** Cooked fish + Queen's Jam + Deer Stew
- **Mead:** 3x Minor Health Mead, 3x Tasty Mead (stamina regen)
- **Optional:** 1x Stagbreaker (for clearing root adds)

### Arena Prep — Critical

This is the first boss where **arena prep matters**. Do this before summoning:

1. **Dig a perimeter trench** — 3m deep, 2m wide, around the altar area. The Elder's root attack can't cross water/deep trenches.
2. **Build pillars** — place 3–4 stone or core-wood pillars around the arena (they block his ranged root attack).
3. **Place a portal** — 100m back, linked to your base.
4. **Build a workbench** — nearby, with a roof, in case you need to repair gear mid-fight.

### Fight Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%–50% HP):**
- The Elder slowly walks toward you. Shoot fire arrows and strafe.
- He summons **vines from the ground** — they deal moderate damage and snare you. Roll through the snare to break it.
- Ranged root volley: he fires a spread of 5–6 root projectiles. Hide behind a pillar.
- When he slams the ground, a shockwave radiates outward — jump over it or roll through.

**Phase 2 (50%–0% HP):**
- Adds **Root Rascals** (small summoned enemies) — ignore them and keep shooting.
- More frequent root volleys. Use pillars aggressively.
- He gains a **charge attack** — he rolls toward you. Sprint sideways.

**Kill strategy:** Kite around the pillars, shooting fire arrows. Each fire arrow does ~35 damage plus 5–10 burn damage per tick. Expect to use 180–220 arrows. The fire DOT prevents him from healing.

**Drop:** Swamp Key. **Power:** +35% Wood Cutting for 5 minutes.

## 🐍 Bonemass — Swamp

A massive, rotting blob of poison and pain. Bonemass is widely considered the hardest boss in the base game — he hits hard, has high HP, and his poison AOE can kill you in seconds without preparation.

### Spawn & Summon

| Item | How to Get |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **10 Withered Bone** | Dropped by skeletons in Sunken Crypts — swim down into the crypts and clear rooms |

Bonemass's altar is a giant skull on a hill in the Swamp. Look for a green glowing aura.

### Absolutely Mandatory Prep

- **Poison Resist Mead** — you MUST drink this before the fight. Without it, Bonemass's poison spit deals 90 damage per tick.
- **Stamina Mead** — 3–4 pots. Bonemass is a marathon fight.
- **Health Mead** — 5+ pots.

### Recommended Gear

| Slot | Best Choice | Good Alternative |
|:-----|:------------|:-----------------|
| **Weapon** | Iron Mace (blunt damage is key) | Stagbreaker (slower but AOE) |
| **Bow** | Huntsman bow | Finewood bow |
| **Armor** | Full iron scale set | Bronze + poison resist |
| **Food** | Serpent Stew + Sausages + Turnip Stew | Any high HP + stamina combo |

### Arena Prep — Mandatory

1. Build a **tree-house platform** in the indestructible Swamp trees (you'll shoot from above).
2. Lay down **workbench + portal** on the platform.
3. Place **campfires** on the ground (they damage Bonemass slightly).
4. Clear the area of Draugr and Leeches before summoning.

### Fight Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%–60% HP):**
- Bonemass walks slowly toward you. Use blunt melee — arrows do **negligible damage** (he resists pierce 90%).
- Poison spit: a green cloud that lingers for 8 seconds. Roll away immediately.
- Arm-swing attack: slow but heavy. Block with iron shield, then counter with 2–3 mace hits.

**Phase 2 (60%–30% HP):**
- He starts **vomiting poison** — a persistent AOE cone in front of him. Never stand in front.
- Summons **Skeleton adds** — kill them with 1–2 mace swings. They drop bones for ammo.
- AOE belly-flop: large radius, high damage. Roll away when he rears up.

**Phase 3 (30%–0% HP):**
- Enraged state — faster attack speed, more frequent poison.
- More skeletons. Consider using Stagbreaker to AOE boss + adds simultaneously.
- Keep poison resist active — reapply if the icon drops.

**Kill strategy:** Stand slightly behind his left flank to avoid the vomit cone. Circle right, hit 2–3 times with the iron mace, roll away. Repeat for 10–15 minutes. Blunt damage is the only effective option — iron mace deals ~55 damage per hit vs. 8 from arrows.

**Drop:** Wishbone (digs up buried treasure). **Power:** +75% Resist Blunt/Pierce for 3 minutes.

## 🔥 Moder — Mountains

A frost-breathing dragon nesting atop the highest mountain peak. The Mountain biome itself is a hazard — expect wolves, drakes, and freezing temperatures.

### Spawn & Summon

| Item | How to Get |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **3 Dragon Tear** | Dropped by Drake nests on mountain peaks — shoot down the nests with arrows |

Moder's altar is on top of a large mountain marked by a stone structure with runes.

### Recommended Gear

| Slot | Best Choice |
|:-----|:------------|
| **Weapon** | Draugr Fang (poison bow, best in slot) or Huntsman bow |
| **Melee backup** | Silver Sword or Frostner |
| **Armor** | Wolf fur chest + root chest (for pierce resist) or full silver |
| **Food** | Eye scream + Wolf Skewer + Lox Pie (max HP ~200) |
| **Mead** | 3x Minor Health, 3x Stamina, 3x Fire Resist (yes, Moder has some fire-adjacent attacks) |

### Arena Prep

1. **Bonfires** — place 3–4 around the peak. The fire helps counteract the freezing.
2. **Workbench + Portal** — essential. You will die. The mountain is dangerous.
3. **Flat ground** — use a hoe to level a large area. Uneven terrain will kill you against Moder's ice breath.
4. **Walls** — build stone walls on the *outside* edges so you don't fall off.

### Fight Strategy

Moder is a flying boss, so ranged weapons are the primary choice.

**Phase 1 (100%–40% HP):**
- Moder circles overhead, blasting ice breath in a wide cone. Sprint perpendicular to the breath.
- She sometimes **lands** — get 4–5 bow shots into her head (critical hit) before she flies again.
- Ice shard volley: she flies up and rains ice shards. Run in a wide circle to avoid.
- When she lands, you can also close to melee — Frostner's slow effect is excellent here.

**Phase 2 (40%–0% HP):**
- She lands **more frequently** but also takes off faster.
- Adds several **Drakes** — small frost dragons that chip at your HP. Ignore them if you can, kill with one arrow each if they swarm.
- Ice storm: she creates a stationary blizzard that deals rapid damage. Move away from the white cloud.

**Kill strategy:** The Draugr Fang bow (poison arrows) is optimal. Each shot deals ~80 damage + 10 poison DOT. You need 250–300 arrows. Kite around the stone walls when she's flying. When she lands, stand behind a pillar, pop out to shoot, duck back.

**Drop:** Dragon Tear (crafts **Artisan Table**). **Power:** Wind always at your back for 3 minutes (faster sailing!).

## 💀 Yagluth — Plains

A giant glowing skeleton sorcerer. Yagluth is a magic-based boss with devastating AOE attacks. The Plains biome is dangerous — even getting to the altar is half the battle.

### Spawn & Summon

| Item | How to Get |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **5 Fuling Totem** | Dropped by Fuling Berserkers and Fuling Shamans in Plains villages |

Yagluth's altar is a massive stone hand emerging from the ground.

### Recommended Gear

| Slot | Best Choice |
|:-----|:------------|
| **Weapon** | Blackmetal Sword or Porcupine (club) |
| **Armor** | Full Padded Iron Set (or Root Set for pierce resist) |
| **Food** | Lox Pie + Bread + Blood Pudding |
| **Mead** | 3x Major Healing, 3x Stamina, 3x **Fire Resist** (critical) |
| **Bow** | Draugr Fang (optional, for pulling adds) |

### Arena Prep — Critical

1. **Clear the entire area** of all Plains mobs — deathsquitos, lox, fuling patrols — within 200m.
2. **Build a stone ring** — 1–2 high, around the altar. This blocks Yagluth's meteor attack.
3. **Place a portal** outside the ring, protected by walls.
4. **Workbench + repair station** inside the ring.
5. **Night-time warning:** Fuling patrols are more active at night. Fight Yagluth at dawn.

### Fight Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%–50% HP):**
- Yagluth hovers slowly, telegraphing each attack with a long wind-up.
- Meteor shower: he raises his hand, and fireballs rain down. Hide behind the stone ring.
- Fire laser: a beam of flame that sweeps the ground. Sprint perpendicular to the beam.
- Hand slam: he slams his fist down, creating a shockwave. Roll through it.

**Phase 2 (50%–0% HP):**
- All attacks speed up. The meteor shower has more meteors in a wider spread.
- He gains a **ground claw** — his hand reaches up from below, grabbing you. Roll when the ground cracks.
- AOE fire pulse: the ground catches fire around him. Back away and wait for it to dissipate.

**Kill strategy:** Learn the telegraphs. Every attack has a 2–3 second wind-up. Dodge the meteors behind the wall, charge in after the laser, land 4–5 sword hits, retreat. Repeat. The fight takes 12–18 minutes. Use the Porcupine club if you want a chance at stagger.

**Drop:** Yagluth Thing (crafts **Spinning Wheel**). **Power:** +75% Resist Magic for 3 minutes.

## 🕯️ The Queen — Mistlands

The Mistlands boss — a giant armored insect. The Mistlands biome itself is the hardest area in the game, requiring extensive preparation just to navigate.

### Spawn & Summon

| Item | How to Get |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **9 Seal Fragment** | Found in Infested Mines and Dvergr towers across the Mistlands |

The Queen's arena is an **Infested Citadel** — a massive structure deep in the Mistlands.

### Recommended Gear

| Slot | Best Choice |
|:-----|:------------|
| **Weapon** | Mistwalker (sword), Krom (greatsword), or Demolisher (2h hammer) |
| **Armor** | Carapace Armor Set (or Mage set for spell builds) |
| **Food** | Misthare Supreme + Honey Glazed Chicken + Bread |
| **Mead** | 5x Major Healing, 5x Stamina, 3x Fire Resist |
| **Wisplight** | Absolutely required — upgrades to Wisp Torch for the fight |

### Arena Prep

1. **Wisps everywhere** — place Wisp Torches around the citadel to pierce the Mistlands fog inside.
2. **Portal** — place one at the citadel entrance. Use two portals (in/out).
3. **Clear the citadel** — kill all Seekers and Ticks inside the citadel before starting the boss fight.
4. **Craft Bonemass Power** — seriously, pop it at the start of the fight.

### Fight Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%–60% HP):**
- The Queen rushes at you with a forward charge. Roll sideways — the charge has huge reach.
- She summons **Seeker adds** (2–3). Kill them immediately or they overwhelm you.
- Spit attack: she spits a corrosive blob. It sticks to the ground and damages over time. Move away.
- Claw combo: 3-hit rapid claw attack. Block the first, roll away from the second and third.

**Phase 2 (60%–30% HP):**
- Summons **Ticks** — small parasites that latch onto you and drain HP. Roll to shake them off.
- The Queen gains a **shield** — a glowing barrier that reduces damage by 50%. Wait for it to fade.
- Ground pound: she slams her tail, creating a shockwave. Jump over it.

**Phase 3 (30%–0% HP):**
- **Enraged** — all attacks are faster, she summons more adds, and the shield appears more often.
- AOE poison cloud: the arena fills with poison. Move to a clear area.
- Desperate charge: she charges repeatedly. Keep a pillar between you and her.

**Kill strategy:** Use the Mistwalker sword (+ spirit damage, great against Mistlands enemies). Kite around the central pillars. Pop Bonemass power at the start for +75% resist. Kill adds immediately. Land 2–3 hits between her attack cycles. Expect 15+ minutes. Carapace arrows from a crossbow can contribute ~300 damage across the fight.

**Drop:** Queen Trophy + Seal Breaker. **Power:** +50% Mining / +5 Comfy for 3 minutes.

## 🔥 Fader — Ashlands

The final boss added in the Ashlands update. Fader is a massive fire-wreathed beast that tests everything you've learned. The Ashlands biome is Valheim's hardest.

### Spawn & Summon

| Item | How to Get |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **1 Dyrnwyn Fragment + 3 Flame Metal** | The fragment is found in the Fortress at the Ashlands center. Flame Metal from Charred enemies |

Fader's altar is on the tallest peak in the Ashlands.

### Recommended Gear

| Slot | Best Choice |
|:-----|:------------|
| **Weapon** | Mistwalker or Ashlands-tier sword (Flame Metal Sword) |
| **Armor** | Ashlands Carapace or full Carapace with fire resist |
| **Food** | Ashlands-tier foods: Boar Jerky + Spicy Bread + Fish Wrap |
| **Mead** | 5x Major Healing, 5x/ Fire Resist Mead (mandatory), Stamina Mead |
| **Bow** | Ashlands crossbow (Spinesnap) with carapace bolts |

### Arena Prep

1. **Bonfires** provide warmth but can also spread fire — place them carefully.
2. **Stone walls** — Fader's fire attacks destroy wood. Stone only.
3. **Portal bunker** — build a fully enclosed stone room for your portal.
4. **Cleanse the area** — kill all Charred enemies within 300m.
5. **Pop Bonemass + Fire Resist mead** before the fight starts.

### Fight Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%–50% HP):**
- Fader walks slowly, spewing fire cones. Dodge laterally.
- Tail whip: a 180-degree arc behind him. Never stand behind him for long.
- He summons **Charred skeletons** — one-shot them or ignore if you have AOE.
- Fire wave: a wall of flame that expands from him. Run away or roll through.

**Phase 2 (50%–20% HP):**
- Fader **flies briefly**, raining fireballs. Sprint in a zigzag.
- Adds a **lava pool** attack — the ground cracks and lava erupts. Keep moving.
- Wing buffet: he flaps, pushing you backward. Brace against a wall.

**Phase 3 (20%–0% HP):**
- **Fully enraged** — double attack speed, near-constant fire AOE.
- Massive firestorm: the entire arena catches fire. Use the Bonemass power.
- Last stand: he charges repeatedly with no cooldown. Pillar-hug and hit 1–2 times between charges.

**Kill strategy:** Fire resist mead + Bonemass power = invulnerability window. Pop both at the start and you'll take 80% reduced damage for 3 minutes. Use that window to deal massive melee damage. Kite around stone pillars. The fight is a DPS race — the longer it goes, the more fire covers the arena. Bring 200+ health meads.

**Drop:** Fader Trophy. **Power:** +100% Fire damage for 5 minutes.
