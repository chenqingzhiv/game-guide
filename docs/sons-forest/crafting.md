# 🏗️ Sons of the Forest — Crafting & Building Guide

*Last updated: June 2026 | Game version: 1.1*

---

Crafting and building in Sons of the Forest has been significantly expanded from the first game. The building system uses a **skeletal structure** — you place logs, planks, and struts to form frames, then fill in walls, floors, and roofs. This guide covers every recipe, building part, and defensive structure.

**Power Grid Tips:**
- Solar panels generate power only during daylight hours
- Batteries store excess power for nighttime use
- Each battery holds enough charge for ~4 hours of moderate use
- Electric fences consume 10 power per minute per section
- Lights consume 2 power per minute per light

## 🔥 Defensive Strategy Guide

### Base Layout Principles

1. **Perimeter Wall** — A single log wall won't stop a mutant. Build **double-layer walls** with a 1m gap between them. Mutants break through the first layer, then have to break the second.
2. **Spike Traps** — Place defensive wall spikes on the OUTSIDE of your perimeter. Cannibals and mutants take damage as they attack the wall.
3. **Kill Corridor** — Build a 2m-wide corridor with defensive spikes on both sides leading to a dead end. Mutants path through it and take damage the entire way.
4. **Elevated Platforms** — Build watchtower platforms 2 stories high. Most mutants can't reach you, and you can shoot down safely.
5. **Multiple Exits** — Always have at least 2 exits. A zipline escape route is ideal.

### Recommended Build Order

```text
Night 1: Tarp shelter + campfire + crafted spear
Day 2:   Small log cabin (3×3), basic door, bed
Day 3:   Perimeter wall (single log), 2 watchtowers
Day 4:   Double-layer wall, reinforced door, spike defenses
Day 5+:  Stone foundations, electric fence, full compound
```

### Mutant-Proofing Checklist

- [ ] Double-layer log walls (8 logs per 2m section)
- [ ] Defensive spikes on outer wall layer
- [ ] Reinforced door (not basic door)
- [ ] Watchtower with clear sightlines
- [ ] Zipline escape route
- [ ] Interior water collector (emergency supply)
- [ ] Storage shed for ammo and armor
- [ ] Multiple bed locations (save points)
