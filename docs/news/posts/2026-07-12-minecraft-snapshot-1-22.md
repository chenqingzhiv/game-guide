## Minecraft 1.22 Snapshot — Desert Biome Overhaul

Mojang Studios has released the **first snapshot for Minecraft 1.22**, and it's a big one. The update focuses entirely on the **Desert biome**, adding new hostile mobs, procedurally generated temple variants, and a significant expansion to the archaeology system introduced back in 1.20 (Trails & Tales).

### New Hostile Mobs

#### The Scorpion

A new hostile arthropod that spawns in deserts and badlands:

- **Behavior** — Scorpions emerge from burrows at night. They patrol a territory of roughly 15 blocks and will aggressively pursue players who approach. During the day, they retreat underground.
- **Attack** — A tail sting that applies the *Venom* status effect. Venom deals 1 HP of damage every 2 seconds for 10 seconds. The effect stacks up to 3 times.
- **Drops** — Scorpion Chitin (used for armor), Scorpion Stinger (potion ingredient for a Venom-tipped arrow recipe).
- **Breeding** — Scorpions can be fed spiders eyes to produce a baby scorpion, which can be tamed as a follow-along pet that attacks hostile mobs.

#### The Dune Stalker

A faster, more dangerous desert-specific variant of the Husk:

- **Behavior** — Unlike standard husks, Dune Stalkers partially burrow into sand during the day, becoming invisible until a player walks within 3 blocks. They erupt from the sand with a brief stagger effect.
- **Combat** — They wield stone swords and can jump 2 blocks high, making wall-based defenses less effective.
- **Burning immunity** — Like husks, they are immune to sunlight damage, but Dune Stalkers also resist fire damage.
- **Drops** — Sand-encrusted bones (used in archaeology recipes) and a rare Dune Stalker Fang (used to craft a Sandstorm Totem that provides brief blindness immunity).

### Procedurally Generated Desert Temples

The most architectural change in 1.22 is the **procedural temple generation system**. Desert temples are no longer identical structures — each generates with:

- **Variable layouts** — 3–8 rooms connected by corridors. Some rooms are loot rooms (with chests), others are trap rooms (pressure plates trigger TNT, arrow dispensers, or collapsing floors).
- **Difficulty tiers** — Temples generate in three tiers. Tier 1 (small, 3 rooms, simple traps). Tier 2 (medium, 5 rooms, redstone puzzles). Tier 3 (large, 7–8 rooms, miniboss encounter).
- **Miniboss — The Temple Guardian** — In Tier 3 temples, a final chamber contains a stone golem entity that awakens when loot is touched. It has 100 HP, shoots projectile shards, and has area-of-effect slam attacks.

### Archaeology Expansion

The 1.22 archaeology overhaul adds:

- **New dig sites** — Desert temples now have dedicated excavation zones with suspicious sand (not just gravel). Each temple has 10–20 suspicious blocks.
- **Desert pottery shards** — 8 new pottery patterns themed around desert wildlife, temple architecture, and ancient lore.
- **Artifacts** — Rare, non-craftable decorative items found only in temple dig sites:
  - **Golden Scarab** — Placeable entity that slowly crawls across surfaces.
  - **Ancient Vessel** — Right-click to release a short particle effect.
  - **Scepter of Sands** — A tool that turns nearby sand into hardened sandstone on right-click.

### Other Changes

- **New enchantment: Sandstrider** — Boots enchantment that increases movement speed on sand by 60%. Obtained via trading with desert village clerics or from temple loot chests.
- **Camel update** — Camels now spawn with colored saddles in desert villages. They can traverse sand with full speed (no slow-down). Breeding camels produces a baby camel after feeding cactus.
- **Desert wells** — Wells now generate with an archaeology dig site underneath, containing a small loot pool of iron nuggets, bones, and rarely a name tag.

### Other Mobs — Sand Worms

A new environmental threat has been spotted in snapshot footage: **Sand Worms**. These are not killable mobs but environmental hazards that burrow through sand:

- **Behavior** — Sand Worms travel in straight lines beneath sand and gravel. They surface for 2 seconds every 10 seconds, destroying any blocks in their path (similar to silverfish but much larger).
- **Interaction** — Walking on sand where a Sand Worm is traveling causes the sand to shake. Players have about 1 second to jump away before the worm surfaces beneath them.
- **Drops** — None (they despawn when surfacing). However, they leave behind tunnels that can be mined for rapid sand removal.
- **Spawning** — Sand Worms spawn naturally in large desert biomes. They despawn when the player moves more than 24 blocks away.

### Archaeology Expansion Details

The archaeology system gets its biggest expansion since 1.20:

- **Suspicious Sand** — Found in desert temples and wells. Uses a brush to extract items. Each block yields 1–2 items before being consumed.
- **New pottery shards — Desert Patterns** — 8 new shard patterns: Scorpion, Pyramid, Sun, Dune, Oasis, Cactus, Camel, Sandstorm. Complete a decorated pot with all 4 of a set to unlock a special decorative effect.
- **Artifact rarity system** — Artifacts now have tiers:
  - **Common** — Clay pots, bone fragments, flint tools.
  - **Uncommon** — Gold scarab, copper ingots, lapis lazuli.
  - **Rare** — Ancient vessel, scepter of sands, enchanted golden apple.
  - **Epic** — Scepter of Sands (unique), Golden Scarab (animated).
- **Brush enchantment** — The brush tool can now be enchanted with Efficiency and Unbreaking at an enchanting table. Efficiency speeds up brushing, Unbreaking extends brush durability.

### Snapshot Experimental Features

Mojang has tagged several features as "experimental" — they may change or be removed before the full 1.22 release:

- **Sand Worm environmental hazard** — Under review for impact on desert exploration.
- **Temple Guardian miniboss** — Being balanced; drop tables may change.
- **Procedural temple generation** — Layout variety may be reduced if generation is too unpredictable.
- **Camel breeding** — May be restricted if it unbalances desert village economies.
