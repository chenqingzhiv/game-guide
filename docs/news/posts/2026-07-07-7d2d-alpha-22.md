## 7 Days to Die Alpha 22 — The Bandit Era Begins

The Fun Pimps have officially pulled back the curtain on **Alpha 22**, the largest gameplay overhaul since the game launched into early access nearly a decade ago. This update finally delivers on the long-promised **bandit AI system**, alongside a complete **vehicle physics rewrite** and a brand-new **endgame zone** that changes how survivors approach late-stage play.

### Bandit Factions — A New Threat

Alpha 22 introduces two distinct bandit factions that roam the Navezgane wasteland:

- **The Scavengers** — Roving scavenger gangs that hunt in small patrols (2–4 members). They are hostile on sight but lightly armed — typically with melee weapons, pipe rifles, and crossbows. Their camps contain lootable supply crates.
- **The Wardogs** — A heavily armed organized faction with fortified compounds. They use automatic weapons, throw dynamite, and deploy traps around their territory. Engaging a Wardog camp is endgame-level content comparable to a horde night.

Bandits exhibit dynamic AI behaviors:

> *"Bandits react to noise, light, and line-of-sight. A gunshot at night can attract a patrol from 150 meters away. They will flank, retreat when injured, and call reinforcements if the fight drags on."* — Madmole, The Fun Pimps

### Vehicle Overhaul — Physics Reworked

Every vehicle in Alpha 22 has been rebuilt from the ground up using a new physics system. Key changes include:

- **Suspension and terrain** — Vehicles now properly track terrain height. No more floating wheels or clipping through rocks.
- **Fuel economy tuning** — Each vehicle now has a distinct fuel consumption curve. The minibike is efficient on roads but guzzles fuel off-road. The 4x4 truck is a reliable workhorse with balanced consumption.
- **Damage and repairs** — Vehicles take damage from hard impacts and zombie attacks. Repair kits can be crafted at a workbench using forged iron, mechanical parts, and duct tape.
- **New mod slots** — Add off-road tires, armor plating, cargo racks, and nitrous boosters.

### New Endgame Zone: The Wasteland City

A massive **radiated urban biome** has been added to the world generation pool. This dense cityscape features:

- **Skyscrapers (12–20 stories)** with rooftop helicopter loot.
- **Underground metro tunnels** connecting building basements — infested with feral zombies that never despawn.
- **Special loot tier** — "Military-grade" weapon and armor crates with unique legendary variants.

### Performance Improvements

Alpha 22 also ships with major engine upgrades:

- **Unity 2022 LTS** — Better GPU instancing, improved occlusion culling.
- **Multi-threaded chunk loading** — Reduced stutter when exploring at high speed.
- **VRAM optimization** — Texture streaming system reduces memory usage by up to 40%.

### New Crafting and Progression Changes

Alpha 22 brings significant changes to the progression curve:

- **Skill book rework** — Skill books are now tiered across three levels (Apprentice, Journeyman, Master). Each perk requires finding the corresponding book series in loot. Master-level books only spawn in the new Wasteland City.
- **New workstations** — The **Mechanic's Bench** (unlocks vehicle mods) and the **Forge of Calamity** (crafts legendary weapon variants) are new mid-to-late-game additions.
- **Legendary weapon crafting** — A new item tier above Quality 6. Legendary weapons have unique passive effects, such as "Bleed on Crit" for blades or "Penetrating Rounds" for rifles. These require materials obtained from bandit camps and the Wasteland City.

### Quality of Life Improvements

- **Stack size increase** — Many common items now stack to 10,000 (up from 6,000). Construction materials (wood, stone, clay, concrete) stack to 50,000.
- **Crafting queue** — The workbench crafting menu now supports queuing up to 100 items at once.
- **Auto-repair** — A toggle in the workbench UI that automatically repairs damaged items using available materials.
- **Map POI filtering** — The map now supports toggling Point of Interest categories (lootable POIs, bandit camps, trader locations, wilderness).

### Trader Updates

Traders have been expanded with new inventories and quest types:

- **Trader Rekt** (Forest biome) — Now carries tier 2 tools and weapons. Offers "Supply Run" quests that reward vehicle parts.
- **Trader Hugh** (Desert biome) — Focuses on ammunition and weapon mods. Offers "Bandit Elimination" quests.
- **Trader Jen** (Snow biome) — Carries medical supplies and armor mods. Offers "Scavenger Hunt" quests for rare components.
- **Trader Bob** (Wasteland City outpost) — New endgame trader. Sells legendary-tier items in exchange for Bandit Tokens (looted from bandit leaders).
