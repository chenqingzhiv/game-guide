# Factorio Space Age: Six Months Later — A Retrospective

It's been six months since Wube Software dropped Factorio: Space Age on an unsuspecting (and very excited) fanbase back in October 2024. If vanilla Factorio was the factory-building equivalent of mastering a single continent, Space Age is the moment you look at the night sky and realize: *there's an entire solar system to industrialize.*

After hundreds of hours across multiple playthroughs, here's where the expansion stands in July 2026, and what it means for engineers both new and veteran.

## A Quick Recap: What Is Space Age?

Space Age isn't a simple content pack — it's a full vertical expansion that extends the game from "launch a rocket" to "turn the Solar System into your factory floor." Instead of ending at the rocket silo, you now build space platforms, visit four new planets, and tackle a brand-new endgame that makes the original feel like the tutorial.

## The Four New Worlds

Each planet is a biome with its own rules, resources, and frustrations — and that's exactly why they work.

**Vulcanus** is the lava planet you'll likely visit first. No trees, no water — just infinite calcite veins and sulfuric acid geysers. The big draw is molten metal processing: direct ore-to-molten casting bypasses the entire belt-and-inserter ballet of traditional smelting. It's a purist's paradise and the first time you'll seriously rethink your entire base layout.

**Fulgora** is a lightning-blasted wasteland covered in ruins that can be recycled into advanced components — but only if you can handle the power outages. The lightning rod system forces you to think about energy storage and grid resilience in a way vanilla never did. Its scrap-recycling loop is also the first real introduction to the Quality system at scale.

**Gleba** is the curveball. A squishy, organic planet where "assemblers" are biochambers that eat nutrients and spoilage is a core mechanic. Your factory output literally *rots*. Belt management takes on a frantic new dimension as you race spoiled products to their next destination before they turn to mush. It's divisive — some players love the pressure, others find it stressful — but it undeniably rewards fast, clean belt design.

**Aquilo** is the final challenge before the victory screen. Everything freezes. Machines need constant heat, and you're operating on a fractal ice landscape with very limited buildable space. It's cramped, cold, and unforgiving — a fitting test before you can launch the victory platform.

## How the Late-Game Meta Shifted

Before Space Age, the "endgame" was infinite mining productivity research and maybe a megabase. Now, the real endgame is **interplanetary logistics**. Your factory is no longer one base — it's a network of specialized outposts connected by space platforms. The question isn't "how many rockets per minute?" but "how do I keep all five surfaces running simultaneously?"

This changes how you build from the ground up. You think in terms of planetary specialization: Gleba makes bioflux, Vulcanus makes foundry-cast plates, Fulgora recycles holmium. The rocket silo goes from "the end" to "just another logistics chest."

## Quality: The Unexpected Revolution

The Quality system was Space Age's stealth killer feature. Every item now has a quality tier (Normal → Uncommon → Rare → Epic → Legendary), and higher-tier machines run faster, modules stack higher, and assemblers require fewer entities per output.

This has a *dramatic* knock-on effect: your factory doesn't just get bigger — it gets *tighter*. Legendary speed modules in a Legendary beacon reduce your entity count by an order of magnitude. The trade-off? Building a quality-upcycling loop is a logistics puzzle of its own, requiring recyclers, circuit conditions, and careful sorting. Quality isn't just a bonus — it's a whole new game layer.

## Space Platforms: Your New Logistics Backbone

Space platforms are simultaneously the expansion's coolest and most frustrating addition. You design them like ships in the platform builder, but they fly *between* planets, crossing asteroid belts that gradually damage your hull. Balancing ammo production, fuel, and cargo capacity within strict weight limits is a puzzle that never gets old — or easy.

The real trick? You're not building one platform. You're building a fleet: haulers, light couriers, and eventually the massive science platform needed for the final victory. Each one needs to be self-sufficient during transit, which means your defensive designs matter just as much as your cargo throughput.

## Is It Worth Starting Fresh in 2026?

**Absolutely.** Space Age is the definitive Factorio experience. The community has had six months to refine blueprints, share platform designs, and iron out early balance wrinkles. The modding scene has caught up in full force — Space Exploration, Krastorio 2, and the other titans have all been updated to play nicely with Wube's expansion.

If you played vanilla Factorio and bounced off because the endgame felt flat, Space Age gives you that "just one more thing" loop from the first rocket all the way to the victory screen.

## Tips for Newcomers to the Expansion

1. **Visit Vulcanus first.** It's the gentlest introduction to off-world logistics. Gleba and Aquilo will punish you if you show up unprepared.
2. **Don't ignore Quality early.** Even a small Uncommon miner or assembler pays dividends. Get your first quality-upcycling loop going before you visit Fulgora.
3. **Automate rocket parts early.** The cargo rocket is your lifeline. Never hand-feed it — a dedicated rocket-part factory keeps your interplanetary supply chain alive.
4. **Embrace circuit networks.** You'll need them for quality sorting, spoilage management on Gleba, and platform cargo control. Circuits aren't optional in Space Age — they're essential.
5. **Expect to rebuild.** Your first space platform *will* get shot down. Your first Fulgora base *will* jam from scrap overflow. That's part of the fun.

## Vanilla vs. Space Age: Head to Head

| Vanilla | Space Age |
|---------|-----------|
| One factory, one planet | Five surfaces, interlinked |
| Rocket = endgame | Rocket = midpoint |
| Single science path | Branching, planet-specific science |
| No quality | 5-tier quality system |
| Simple logistics bots | Space platform fleet management |
| Your base | Your civilization |

## Final Thoughts

Six months in, Space Age is everything I wanted and more I didn't know I needed. It doesn't replace vanilla — it *completes* it. The base game was always about scaling up. Space Age is about scaling *out*, in every direction, until your factory touches four other worlds.

Factorio was already a masterpiece. Space Age made it a monument.
