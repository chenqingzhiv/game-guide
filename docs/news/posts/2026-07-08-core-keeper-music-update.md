## Core Keeper: Songs of the Core Update

Pugstorm has released the **Music Update** for Core Keeper, bringing **20 original tracks** composed by [insert composer name or studio] alongside a complete ambient audio rework. The update, titled *Songs of the Core*, transforms the game's soundscape from functional background music into a dynamic audio experience that reacts to player actions.

### What's In the Update

#### 20 Brand-New Tracks

Each biome in Core Keeper now has its own suite of music that evolves as you explore deeper:

- **The Dirt Biome** — Warm, percussive tracks with acoustic guitar and hand drums. Four variations cycle based on whether you're near water, in a tunnel, or in an open cavern.
- **The Clay Caves** — Low, rumbling cello lines and muffled percussion. Players report an oppressive, claustrophobic feel that matches the environment perfectly.
- **The Forgotten Ruins** — Echoing piano melodies with reverb-drenched synths. The music here incorporates fragments of the game's main theme, distorted and decayed.
- **The Sunken Sea** — Ethereal choir pads and waterphone (a real instrument played by the composer). The audio team recorded actual underwater ambience and layered it into the mix.
- **The Molten Quarry** — Aggressive percussion and distorted bass. Tempo increases as you approach lava pools.
- **Passage and Azeos' Wilderness** — Orchestral swells with brass sections. These tracks were recorded with a 12-piece chamber ensemble rather than synthesized.

#### Dynamic Audio System

The update introduces a new **procedural audio layer** that blends ambient sounds, creature calls, and music based on context:

- **Combat** — When you engage an enemy, the music subtly shifts to incorporate tension-building percussion. The transition is seamless — no hard cuts.
- **Discovery** — Finding a hidden treasure room triggers a short musical "reveal" motif. Each major boss has its own unique sting.
- **Base-building** — Spending time near your base gradually introduces a relaxed, campfire-style variant of the biome theme. The game tracks how long you've been in your base and blends accordingly.

### Composer Interview Highlights

We spoke with the composer about the creative process behind the soundtrack:

> *"Core Keeper's world is vast and mysterious. I wanted the music to feel like it was carved out of the same stone as the caves — organic, resonant, and a little bit lonely. Each biome got its own harmonic palette that represents the minerals and life forms found there."*

The composer revealed that the **Clay Caves theme** was written after a day-long field recording session in an actual limestone cave system:

> *"I recorded over 200 different drips, echoes, and footsteps in a real cave. Those sounds became the rhythmic foundation for the entire biome's music."*

### Player Reactions

The update has been warmly received by the Core Keeper community:

> *"The way combat music blends in is incredible. No more jarring cuts — it just flows."* — Reddit r/CoreKeeper

### How to Access

The Songs of the Core update is available now on **Steam, GOG, and Xbox Game Pass**. It's a free update for all existing players — no DLC purchase required.

### What's Next?

Pugstorm has confirmed this is part of a larger **audio quality-of-life initiative**. Future updates will include:

- **Voice-acted cutscenes** for major story beats
- **Environmental radio stations** (in-game devices that play lore-friendly broadcasts)
- **Custom jukebox** allowing players to craft record discs collected from the world

### Accessibility Settings

The update also introduces an **Accessibility Audio Menu**:

- **Separate volume sliders** — Combat music, ambient SFX, creature sounds, and UI sounds can be individually adjusted.
- **Music fade toggle** — Disable dynamic music transitions if you prefer the old sudden-switch behavior.
- **Mono audio mode** — For players with hearing in one ear, a mono downmix option collapses stereo audio to a single channel.
- **Visual sound indicator** — Subtle screen-edge pulses for significant audio cues (boss spawns, treasure discovery, alert sounds).

### Performance Impact

Pugstorm confirmed the new audio system has minimal CPU overhead. Testing shows:

- **PC (recommended specs)** — Less than 2% CPU usage increase.
- **Steam Deck** — Runs at native audio quality with no frame rate impact.
- **Nintendo Switch** — Audio quality is slightly compressed (48 kHz → 32 kHz) but dynamic transitions and all 20 tracks are present.

### How the Update Rolled Out

The Music Update was released in two phases:

1. **Phase 1 (June 22)** — Audio engine update (required download of new audio middleware). Players had 24 hours to download before multiplayer compatibility split.
2. **Phase 2 (June 23)** — Live launch of the 20 tracks and dynamic system. Simultaneous release on all platforms via a 1.2 GB patch.

Players who missed Phase 1 can still update directly to the current version — both phases are packaged together.

### What Players Are Building

The community has already started sharing creative uses of the new audio system:

- **Concert halls** — Players are building amphitheaters near biome borders where the music dynamically shifts as you walk through different zones.
- **Audio landmarks** — Treasure rooms and hidden locations marked by their unique musical stingers have become pilgrimage sites for exploration parties.
- **Sound-based navigation** — Some speedrunners are using the audio cues to navigate the Sunken Sea, where visual landmarks are scarce.
