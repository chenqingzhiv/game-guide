## Grounded 2 Officially Teased by Obsidian

Obsidian Entertainment has sent the survival gaming community into a frenzy with a **cryptic social media teaser** that strongly suggests **Grounded 2** is in development. The teaser, posted across the studio's official channels, shows a close-up macro photograph of an ant carrying a tiny piece of paper with the Obsidian logo — accompanied by the caption *"Some adventures are too big to stay small."*

### What We Know

While Obsidian hasn't made a formal announcement, several pieces of evidence point to a sequel being well underway:

**The social media teaser (June 30, 2026):**

The image shows a carpenter ant viewed from below — a perspective Grounded players will recognize from the game's camera angle. The paper fragment carried by the ant reads *"File: GND-2 — Classification: Active Development."* Fans quickly zoomed in and noticed the timestamp on the paper reads *"09:24 — 14 June 2026."*

**Senior developer hints:**

In a recent interview with *PC Gamer*, Grounded's lead narrative designer was asked about the possibility of a sequel:

> *"I can't say anything specific, but I will say the world of the backyard surprised even us. There are corners we never got to explore — other yards, interior spaces, seasonal environments. The insect world is a lot bigger than one backyard."*

**Job listings:**

Obsidian posted three job openings in late May that reference an unannounced survival game:

- Senior Environmental Artist — *"Experience with non-human scale environments preferred"*
- Combat Designer — *"Working knowledge of insect and arachnid behaviors considered a plus"*
- Technical Animator — *"Rigging for non-humanoid limb structures required"*

These listings were removed after 72 hours — typical for projects Obsidian isn't ready to discuss publicly.

### What Could Grounded 2 Look Like?

Based on the hints, community speculation centers on several potential directions:

#### 1. Multiple Yard Biomes

The original Grounded was set in a single backyard. A sequel could expand to **multiple connected outdoor environments**:

- **The Vegetable Garden** — Tall stalks, soft soil, irrigation puddles, and gardening tools as obstacles.
- **The Compost Heap** — A constantly-shifting biome of decomposing material with new insect types.
- **The Shed Interior** — An indoor environment with dust motes, chemical hazards, and spider webs.
- **The Park** — A public space with different rules — more open, less shelter.

#### 2. Seasonal Weather System

Obsidian's narrative designer hinted at **seasonal environments**. A dynamic weather system could change the world state:

- **Spring** — Rain puddles create new waterways and drown low-lying areas. Bees become active.
- **Summer** — Hot concrete forces movement onto shaded grass paths. Ants migrate.
- **Autumn** — Falling leaves create temporary bridges and cover. New mushroom types spawn.
- **Winter** — Frost limits insect activity above ground. Underground exploration becomes essential.

#### 3. Cooperative Cross-Save

Grounded's success on Xbox Game Pass and Steam makes cross-progression a natural next step. Obsidian's parent company Microsoft would likely push for full cross-save between PC and console.

### What Obsidian Has Said Officially

When approached for comment, an Obsidian spokesperson gave a standard non-answer:

> *"We're always exploring new ideas at Obsidian. Grounded remains an important part of our studio's legacy. We have nothing to announce at this time."*
