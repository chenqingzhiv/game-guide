## 🎯 What is Timberborn?

Timberborn is a **post-apocalyptic beaver city-builder** where you lead a colony of intelligent beavers rebuilding civilisation after humanity's collapse. Water is scarce, droughts are relentless, and every structure you build sits in a beautifully voxel-rendered world with a fully simulated water physics system.

**Why it stands out:**

- **Realistic water physics** — rivers flow, evaporate, and flood in real-time. Your survival depends on taming it.
- **Vertical construction** — build upwards with platforms, stilts, and multi-level districts.
- **Two distinct beaver factions** — Folktails (traditional, nature-friendly) vs Ironteeth (industrial, tech-focused), each with unique buildings and playstyles.
- **Deep seasonal cycles** — manage water reserves during multi-day droughts, then rebuild when the wet season returns.

> 💡 **Timberborn 1.0 launched March 21, 2026**, adding the full campaign, new wonders, faction-specific win conditions, and the final tech tree. The June 2026 update introduced additional quality-of-life features and balance adjustments.

## 💧 Water Management — The Core Mechanic

Water is **the most important resource** in Timberborn. Without it, your beavers die in 3–5 days. Understanding the water system separates thriving colonies from ghost towns.

### Key Structures

| Structure | Cost | Function | Unlock |
|:----------|:-----|:---------|:-------|
| **Levee** | 4 Logs | Blocks water flow; cannot pass water at all | Start |
| **Dam** | 6 Logs (or metal variants) | Blocks water but allows overflow when submerged | Start |
| **Floodgate** | 8 Logs + 1 Plank | Adjustable barrier; can be opened/closed to control water release | Mid-game |
| **Water Pump** | 10 Logs + 2 Planks | Extracts drinkable water from rivers or reservoirs | Start (small) |
| **Deep Water Pump** | 15 Logs + 4 Planks | Pumps from deeper water sources; more drought-resistant | Late-game (Ironteeth) |
| **Mechanical Pump** | 10 Logs + 3 Planks + 3 Gears | Powered pump — moves water uphill; requires power connection | Mid-game (Ironteeth) |

### Water Strategy Tips

1. **Build a reservoir before your first drought.** Identify a natural valley or canyon, and block the downstream end with levees and a dam. The upstream area will fill during the wet season.
2. **Place floodgates at the bottom of your dam.** During drought, close them to conserve water. Open them at the start of wet season to refresh your reservoir.
3. **Elevation matters.** Water flows downhill — always build your pumps at the lowest accessible point of your reservoir.
4. **Mechanical pumps change everything.** The Ironteeth can pump water *upwards*, enabling rooftop reservoirs and vertical farming. This is a massive mid-game advantage.
5. **Evaporation is real.** During long droughts (10+ days), shallow water disappears. A deep, narrow reservoir evaporates slower than a wide, shallow one.

## 🏗️ Vertical Building

Timberborn's water physics means you can't always build outward — you **must** build upward.

> 🌊 *"Water finds its level. Your city shouldn't."*

### Platforms & Stilts

| Structure | Cost | Purpose |
|:----------|:-----|:--------|
| **Platform** | 2 Logs | Walkable floor tile at a raised elevation |
| **Stilt** | 4 Logs | Supports a platform above ground, leaving space underneath |
| **Staircase** | 6 Logs | Connects two elevation levels; diagonal movement |

### Vertical Building Tips

- **Build housing on platforms** above flood-risk areas. Ground-level buildings get washed away.
- **Stack workshops vertically** — place production buildings on raised platforms and use chutes to drop goods downward.
- **Underslung storage** — place platforms high enough to walk under, then store resources underneath.
- **Roof reservoirs** — with mechanical pumps (Ironteeth), create water reserves on rooftops for gravity-fed irrigation.
- **Elevator shafts** (late-game, both factions) allow vertical transport of goods between levels.

## 🌾 Food Chain

Your beavers need a steady food supply. The food chain starts simple and branches as you unlock crops, cooking, and speciality goods.

### Tier 1 — Basic Starvation Prevention

| Crop | Growth Days | Yield per Tile | Notes |
|:-----|:------------|:---------------|:------|
| **Carrots** | 5 | 3 | Default starting food. Dirt-cheap, plant anywhere |
| **Berries** | 7 | 5 (Folktails +2) | Wild bushes; Folktails get bonus yield |
| **Cattails** | 6 | 4 | Ironteeth staple; grows in shallow water |
| **Mushrooms** | 4 | 3 | Ironteeth-only; grows in dark/damp areas |

### Tier 2 — Cooked Meals

Once you research **Grill** or **Cooking Station**, combine ingredients for better nutrition:

| Meal | Ingredients | Nutritional Value | Happiness Bonus |
|:-----|:------------|:-----------------|:----------------|
| **Grilled Carrots** | 2 Carrots | 1.5× basic | — |
| **Berry Pancakes** | 3 Berries + 2 Flour | 3× basic | +1 |
| **Mushroom Soup** | 2 Mushrooms + 1 Water | 2× basic | +0.5 |
| **Bread** | 3 Flour (wheat) | 2× basic | +0.5 |
| **Beer** (Folktails) | 4 Carrots → Brewery | — | +2 Happiness |
| **Kelp Sushi** | 2 Kelp + 1 Berry | 3× basic | +1.5 |

### Water — Don't Forget

Beavers drink **1 unit of water per beaver per day**. A colony of 20 beavers needs 20 water/day. During a 10-day drought, that's 200 stored water — minimum.

### Food Chain Progression

```text
Early Game (Days 1–15)   → Carrots + Berries (gathered)
Mid Game (Days 15–50)    → Grills + Basic farms + Water reservoirs
Late Game (Days 50+)     → Cooked meals + Speciality goods + Beer
End Game (1.0+)          → Full automated food chains + Wonders
```

## 📋 5 Beginner Tips

| # | Tip | Why It Matters |
|:--|:----|:---------------|
| 1 | **Start with Folktails.** Their wood economy and forgiving drought mechanics make the first 50 days much smoother. Save Ironteeth for your second playthrough. | Learning water management is hard enough without industrial complexity. |
| 2 | **Build a dam and reservoir before Day 6.** The first drought hits around Day 10–15. You need at least 50 units of stored water by then for a colony of 10 beavers. | Running out of water = colony death in 3–5 days. No exceptions. |
| 3 | **Don't overpopulate early.** Keep your population at 8–12 beavers until you have stable food + water reserves for 10 days. | More beavers = more mouths. Grow only when you have surplus. |
| 4 | **Leave space between buildings.** Timberborn has no deletion penalty, but rebuilding costs time. Leave 1-tile gaps for future paths, stairs, and water channels. | You will need to expand. Tight layouts become impossible to modify. |
| 5 | **Observe your first drought.** Pause the game, zoom out, and watch where water pools, where it disappears first, and where your beavers walk. Your second colony will be 10× better. | The simulation is educational. Every failed colony teaches you water physics. |
