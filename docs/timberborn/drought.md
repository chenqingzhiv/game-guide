# Timberborn Drought Survival Guide

Droughts are the central challenge in Timberborn. As the life-giving water recedes, your beaver colony must rely on stored resources, efficient infrastructure, and smart planning to survive until the next wet season. This guide covers everything you need to know about surviving droughts, from basic water management to advanced district planning and faction-specific strategies.

For detailed reference pages, also check our [Water Management Guide](../timberborn/water.md), [Food Production Guide](../timberborn/food.md), [Beaver Types](../timberborn/beavers.md), [Buildings Database](../timberborn/buildings.md), and [District Planning](../timberborn/districts.md).

## Understanding the Drought Cycle

Timberborn operates on a cycle of wet and dry seasons. The length and severity of droughts increase over time, making early preparation critical.

| Season Type | Typical Duration (Early Game) | Typical Duration (Late Game) | Water Availability |
|-------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------|
| Wet Season  | 3–7 days                      | 5–10 days                     | Rivers flow, rain falls |
| Drought     | 3–10 days                     | 15–30+ days                   | No rain, rivers dry up |

**Key Insight:** The first drought usually arrives around day 10–15. You must have water stored before then.

## Water Management: The Lifeline of Your Colony

Water is the most critical resource during a drought. Without it, your beavers die of thirst, crops fail, and industry grinds to a halt.

### 1. Building Water Storage

You have two primary methods for storing water: **water tanks** and **reservoirs** (dams).

**Water Tanks (Small & Large)**

| Structure | Capacity | Materials | Best Use |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Small Water Tank | 50 units | 10 Logs, 4 Planks | Early game, scattered storage |
| Large Water Tank | 200 units | 20 Logs, 10 Planks, 4 Gears | Centralized storage for districts |

**Strategy:** Place water tanks near farms and housing. A single Large Water Tank can support 10–15 beavers for a short drought, but you'll need multiple for longer events.

**Reservoirs (Dams & Levees)**

Reservoirs are the most efficient way to store massive amounts of water. By damming a river, you create a lake that holds thousands of units.

- **Levees:** Stackable blocks to raise walls.
- **Floodgates:** Controllable gates to release water when needed.
- **Dams:** Water-permeable structures that slow flow.

**How to Build a Reservoir:**

1. Identify a narrow section of river.
2. Build a wall of levees across the river, leaving a gap for a floodgate.
3. Place a floodgate in the gap (set to 1.0–2.0 meters height).
4. During wet season, water fills the reservoir behind the dam.
5. During drought, close the floodgate completely to preserve water.

**Pro Tip:** Build your reservoir upstream from your main colony. Gravity will help distribute water downstream when you open the floodgates.

### 2. Water Distribution Systems

Once you have stored water, you need to get it to your beavers.

**Mechanical Water Pumps**

| Pump Type | Range | Power | Output |
|-----------|-------|-------|--------|
| Manual Water Pump | 1 tile radius | None (beaver operated) | 1 unit per trip |
| Mechanical Water Pump | 3 tile radius | 1 HP | 5 units per cycle |

- **Manual Pumps:** Essential early game. Place one near every water source.
- **Mechanical Pumps:** Require power (water wheels or windmills). They can fill tanks automatically and are vital for large colonies.

**Irrigation Towers**

Irrigation towers keep your crops alive during droughts. They consume water but are essential for food production.

| Tower Type | Water Consumption | Radius | Duration |
|------------|-------------------|--------|----------|
| Small Irrigation Tower | 1 unit per hour | 5 tiles | Continuous |
| Large Irrigation Tower | 2 units per hour | 10 tiles | Continuous |

**Strategy:** Place irrigation towers near your farms. During a drought, they will keep the ground moist, allowing crops to grow. Always ensure your irrigation towers have a dedicated water supply.

### 3. Water Conservation Tips

- **Close floodgates early:** Don't wait until the river is dry. Close floodgates when the water level drops below 50% of your reservoir capacity.
- **Use deep reservoirs:** A 3-tile-deep reservoir holds three times more water than a 1-tile-deep one of the same surface area.
- **Prioritize drinking water:** Irrigation towers can be turned off if water is scarce. Beavers need drinking water first.
- **Build multiple small reservoirs:** A single large reservoir is a single point of failure. Multiple smaller reservoirs provide redundancy.

## Food Reserves: Stockpiling for the Dry Season

Beavers eat constantly. Without food, they starve in 3–5 days. You need to stockpile enough food to last the entire drought.

### 1. Food Types and Efficiency

| Food Item | Ingredients | Nutrition per Unit | Spoilage | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|--------------------|----------|----------|
| Carrots | 1 Carrot (grown) | 1 | None | Early game, basic survival |
| Berries | 1 Berry bush | 0.5 | None | Quick food source |
| Potatoes | 1 Potato (grown) | 2 | None | High nutrition, late game |
| Grilled Potatoes | 1 Potato + 1 Log | 3 | None | Best nutrition per unit |
| Bread | 2 Wheat + 1 Log | 4 | None | Endgame staple |
| Maple Syrup | 1 Maple sap | 2 | None | Luxury food, boosts happiness |

**Key Insight:** Grilled Potatoes are the most efficient food for drought survival. One potato becomes three nutrition, and logs are abundant.

### 2. Building Food Reserves

**Food Storage Buildings**

| Building | Capacity | Materials | Notes |
|----------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Small Food Storage | 50 units | 10 Logs, 4 Planks | Early game |
| Large Food Storage | 200 units | 20 Logs, 10 Planks, 4 Gears | Centralized storage |
| Underground Food Storage | 300 units | 30 Logs, 15 Planks, 6 Gears | No spoilage, best for long droughts |

**Strategy:** Build at least one Large Food Storage per 20 beavers. Fill it completely before the first drought.

### 3. Crop Rotation and Farming

During a drought, crops stop growing unless irrigated. Plan your farming accordingly.

- **Pre-drought planting:** Plant extra crops 5 days before the expected drought. They will mature just as the drought begins.
- **Irrigated farming:** Use irrigation towers to keep a small farm operational during the drought. This provides fresh food for extended droughts.
- **Multi-crop strategy:** Grow carrots (fast), potatoes (efficient), and wheat (for bread). This diversifies your food supply.

**Example Farm Layout for 20 Beavers:**

- 10 tiles of carrots (emergency food)
- 10 tiles of potatoes (primary food)
- 5 tiles of wheat (for bread)
- 2 berry bushes (snacks)

This produces enough food to sustain 20 beavers for 15 days with proper storage.

## District Planning: Scaling Your Colony

As your colony grows, you'll need to establish multiple districts. Each district must be self-sufficient during droughts.

### 1. District Basics

A district is a separate area with its own storage, housing, and production. Districts are connected by **District Gates** and **Distribution Posts**.

| Structure | Function | Cost |
|-----------|----------|------|
| District Gate | Marks district boundary | 10 Logs, 4 Planks |
| Distribution Post | Transfers goods between districts | 20 Logs, 10 Planks, 4 Gears |

**Why Use Districts?**

- **Resource specialization:** One district can focus on farming, another on industry.
- **Population management:** Prevents overcrowding and housing shortages.
- **Drought resilience:** If one district's water fails, others can survive.

### 2. Designing Drought-Resistant Districts

Each district should have:

- **Independent water supply:** At least one reservoir or water tank.
- **Food production:** Farms and irrigation towers.
- **Storage:** Enough food and water for the district's population for 20+ days.
- **Power:** Water wheels or windmills for mechanical pumps.

**District Size Guidelines:**

| District Type | Population | Water Storage | Food Storage | Notes |
|---------------|------------|---------------|--------------|-------|
| Farming District | 10–15 beavers | 2 Large Water Tanks | 2 Large Food Storages | Focus on crops |
| Industrial District | 15–20 beavers | 3 Large Water Tanks | 1 Large Food Storage | Focus on production |
| Central Hub | 20–30 beavers | 1 Reservoir + 4 Large Tanks | 3 Large Food Storages | Administrative center |

### 3. Distribution During Drought

Use Distribution Posts to move water and food between districts. Set up routes before the drought hits.

**Distribution Priority:**

1. Water (highest priority)
2. Food
3. Logs and planks
4. Other resources

**Pro Tip:** Build a dedicated "Emergency Storage" district with massive water and food reserves. Connect it to all other districts via distribution routes.

## Iron Teeth vs Folktails: Faction Drought Strategies

Each faction has unique strengths and weaknesses when it comes to drought survival.

### Iron Teeth Strategy

**Strengths:**
- **Underground storage:** Iron Teeth have access to Underground Water Tanks and Underground Food Storages, which are immune to evaporation and spoilage.
- **Mechanical advantage:** Their buildings are more power-efficient.
- **Deep reservoirs:** They can build deeper reservoirs with fewer materials.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Higher water consumption:** Iron Teeth beavers drink more water per day.
- **Slower reproduction:** Population growth is slower.

**Iron Teeth Drought Plan:**

1. **Build underground storage immediately.** Prioritize Underground Water Tanks (500 units each) and Underground Food Storages (300 units each).
2. **Create deep reservoirs.** Use levees to create 4-tile-deep reservoirs. This maximizes water storage per surface area.
3. **Use mechanical pumps.** Iron Teeth's Mechanical Water Pump is more efficient. Power it with water wheels.
4. **Focus on potatoes.** Grilled Potatoes are the best food for Iron Teeth due to their high nutrition.
5. **District specialization.** Iron Teeth excel at industrial districts. Keep farming districts small and efficient.

**Iron Teeth Recommended Build Order:**

1. Day 1–5: Housing, basic farms, manual pumps
2. Day 5–10: Underground Water Tank, Large Food Storage
3. Day 10–15: Reservoir with floodgates, mechanical pump
4. Day 15+: Expand storage, build second district

### Folktails Strategy

**Strengths:**
- **Lower water consumption:** Folktails beavers drink less water.
- **Faster reproduction:** Population grows quickly.
- **Better farming:** Folktails have access to more crop types and faster growth.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Above-ground storage:** Their storage is vulnerable to evaporation and spoilage.
- **Less efficient industry:** Requires more power for the same output.

**Folktails Drought Plan:**

1. **Build large above-ground reservoirs.** Folktails can build massive reservoirs using levees and dams. Focus on width rather than depth.
2. **Use irrigation towers extensively.** Folktails' crops grow faster with irrigation. Keep farms running during droughts.
3. **Diversify food sources.** Grow carrots, potatoes, wheat, and berries. This provides redundancy.
4. **Build multiple small districts.** Folktails' fast reproduction means you'll need more housing and food production.
5. **Use windmills for power.** Folktails have efficient windmills that work during droughts (wind is constant).

**Folktails Recommended Build Order:**

1. Day 1–5: Housing, berry bushes, manual pumps
2. Day 5–10: Large Water Tank, Large Food Storage, irrigation tower
3. Day 10–15: Reservoir with multiple floodgates, windmill-powered pumps
4. Day 15+: Expand farms, build new districts, add more storage

## Beginner Tips for Surviving Your First Drought

1. **Start storing water on day 1.** Build a small water tank and fill it immediately. Don't wait.
2. **Count your beavers.** You need 1 unit of water per beaver per day. For a 10-day drought with 15 beavers, you need 150 units of water.
3. **Build extra farms.** Plant 50% more crops than you think you need. Overproduction is better than starvation.
4. **Use floodgates.** They are the most important structure for water management. Learn to use them early.
5. **Don't expand during drought.** Focus on survival. New construction can wait until the wet season.
6. **Keep logs stockpiled.** You need logs for pumps, tanks, and repairs. A shortage of logs during drought can be fatal.
7. **Monitor your water levels.** Check your tanks and reservoirs daily. If water drops below 50%, start rationing.
8. **Build a second district early.** Even a small outpost with a water tank and food storage can save your colony if the main district fails.
9. **Use the pause button.** If a drought is coming and you're not ready, pause non-essential buildings to save resources.
10. **Learn from failure.** Your first colony might die. That's okay. Each failure teaches you something about water management.

## Advanced Strategy: Surviving Mega-Droughts

Late-game droughts can last 30+ days. These require massive preparation.

### Pre-Drought Checklist (30 days before)

- [ ] Build 3+ reservoirs with total capacity of 10,000+ units
- [ ] Stockpile 5,000+ units of food
- [ ] Construct 10+ Large Water Tanks
- [ ] Build 5+ irrigation towers
- [ ] Establish 3+ districts, each self-sufficient
- [ ] Create emergency distribution routes
- [ ] Power all pumps with renewable energy (water wheels or windmills)
- [ ] Build underground storage for Iron Teeth or massive above-ground storage for Folktails

### During the Drought

1. **Close all floodgates immediately.**
2. **Turn off non-essential buildings** (factories, decorations).
3. **Ration water:** Set irrigation towers to minimum height (0.5 meters) to conserve water.
4. **Prioritize drinking water:** If water runs low, turn off all irrigation and focus on beaver hydration.
5. **Use emergency food:** If food runs low, harvest all crops (even unripe ones) and distribute from emergency storage.

### Post-Drought Recovery

1. **Open floodgates slowly.** Let water flow back into dry riverbeds.
2. **Rebuild farms immediately.** Plant fast-growing crops like carrots first.
3. **Refill storage.** Prioritize water tanks and food storage.
4. **Repair damaged buildings.** Floodgates and pumps may need repairs.
5. **Expand storage.** Use the lessons from the drought to build more capacity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When does the first drought hit?**
A: The first drought usually arrives around day 10-15. You should have at least one water tank and food storage built before then.

**Q: How much water do I need per beaver per day?**
A: Folktails drink ~1.5 units per beaver per day; Iron Teeth drink ~2 units. For a 10-day drought with 20 beavers, plan for 300-400 units of stored water.

**Q: Can crops grow during a drought?**
A: Only if irrigated. Use irrigation towers to keep farmland moist. Without irrigation, crops stop growing and die.

**Q: Should I build one big reservoir or several small ones?**
A: Several small reservoirs are safer. A single large reservoir is a single point of failure. Multiple smaller ones provide redundancy.

**Q: Which faction is better for drought survival?**
A: Iron Teeth (9/10) edge out Folktails (8/10) due to underground storage, but Folktails' faster reproduction and lower water consumption make them more forgiving for beginners.

**Q: What food is most efficient for droughts?**
A: Grilled Potatoes (1 potato + 1 log = 3 nutrition) is the most efficient. See the [Food Guide](food.md) for full details.
