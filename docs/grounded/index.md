# Grounded 🐞

**Shrunk to the size of an ant.** Survive the hostile micro-world of a suburban backyard.

[![Grounded Banner](/assets/images/grounded/library_hero.jpg){: .game-hero }](/assets/images/grounded/library_hero.jpg)

## What is Grounded?

Grounded is a cooperative survival game developed by Obsidian Entertainment. You play as one of four teens shrunk to the size of an ant, stranded in a sprawling backyard that is now your entire world. What looks like a simple garden from a human perspective is a dense, dangerous wilderness when you're an inch tall — dewdrops are water tanks, grass blades are skyscrapers, and a wolf spider is a monster the size of a bus.

The game launched in full in September 2022 after two years of Early Access and has received continuous content updates, including the massive "Fully Yoked" update that added NG+, new creatures, and the final story chapter.

## Getting Started Guide

### 1. First Steps — The First 30 Minutes

You spawn near the **Mysterious Machine** in the middle of the backyard. Don't panic — you have a basic quest compass that points to key objectives. Here's your survival checklist:

1. **Craft a Pebblet Axe** (3 pebblets + 2 plant fiber) — your first tool for chopping grass
2. **Craft a Pebblet Hammer** (3 pebblets + 2 plant fiber + 2 woven fiber) — needed for acorn shells and rocks
3. **Harvest plant fiber** from small weeds — this is your most-used resource early on
4. **Gather clover leaves and aphid honey** for basic food
5. **Find a water source** — dewdrops on grass blades and leaves contain clean water
6. **Build a lean-to** (5 grass planks + 4 plant fiber + 3 woven fiber) for a save point and respawn
7. **Craft a torch** — the yard is terrifying at night. Torches keep light orb weavers at a distance

### 3. Biome Progression Order

Grounded's yard is divided into distinct biomes, each with tougher creatures and better resources. Follow this order for a manageable difficulty curve:

```text
Meadows / Starting Grasslands (Level 1–4)
  → Hedge (Level 4–6) — finish the Hedge Lab
    → Pond (Level 5–8) — complete Pond Lab
      → Haze (Level 6–9) — navigate the Haze Lab
        → Sandbox / Shed Surroundings (Level 8–11) — Black Anthill Lab
          → Upper Yard (Level 11–14) — endgame creatures
            → Moldorc Highlands / Castle (Level 14+) — final story
```

Each biome requires better gear. Don't enter the Hedge without an acorn shovel and a bow. Don't attempt the Upper Yard until you have tier-3 tools and mint-malleable armor.

### 4. Resource Gathering Tips

- **Grass planks** are everywhere but heavy — you can only carry 5 at a time. Build a grass plank pallet at your base for storage.
- **Stems** come from dandelions, sprigs, and berry bushes. Needed for scaffolding and advanced buildings.
- **Acorn shells** are your primary early armor — hit acorns with a hammer to crack them open.
- **Quartzite** (red, blue, and green variants) is used for weapon and tool upgrades. Red Quartzite is found in the Hedge, Blue in the Pond depths, Green in the Upper Yard.
- **Bug parts** — every insect drops unique components. Ant parts for early armor, ladybug parts for mid-game armor, and black ox beetle parts for endgame.
- **Buried treasure** — use the Compass or treasure map to find buried acorn bits, quarts, and rare crafting items near landmarks.

### 5. Survival Essentials

| Threat | How to Handle |
|:-------|:--------------|
| **Wolf Spider** | Avoid at all costs early game. Use the crossbow from high ground if you must engage. Patrols the oak tree area. |
| **Orb Weaver** | Spiders that patrol the grasslands and Hedge. Learn perfect blocking — 3-hit combo then a 2-second pause. |
| **Soldier Ant** | Aggressive if you approach the anthill. Don't build near ant patrol routes. |
| **Stinkbug** | Emits poison gas clouds. Wear gas mask (from Pond lab) or use ranged attacks. |
| **Mosquito** | Fast, can one-shot an unprepared player. Use a bow — they have a predictable flight pattern. |
| **Larva** | Common in the Haze. Fire damage — carry a canteen of water to extinguish burning. |
| **Fire Ants** | Upper Yard only. Their bites stack a burning debuff. Use fire ant armor or keep moving. |

**Golden Rule of Early-Game Grounded:** If it's bigger than you, assume it can kill you in 2–3 hits. Sneak, observe, and learn attack patterns before you commit to combat.
