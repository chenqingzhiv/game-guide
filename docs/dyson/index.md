## 🎯 What Is Dyson Sphere Program?

**Dyson Sphere Program (DSP)** is a 3D factory automation game that drops you onto a procedurally generated star cluster with a single goal: **build a Dyson sphere** — a megastructure that harnesses the full energy output of a star.

You play as an AI mecha, crash-landed on a remote planet with just a mining drill and a dream. What begins with manually mining iron ore expands into interplanetary logistics networks, automated space factories, and finally the construction of a star-encompassing energy grid that would make Freeman Dyson proud.

### What Makes DSP Unique?

| Feature | Why It Stands Out |
|:--------|:-----------------|
| **True 3D space** | Build on spherical planets, not flat grids. Belts curve with the terrain. |
| **Interplanetary scale** | Fly between planets in real time. Resources are spread across entire star systems. |
| **Visible Dyson sphere** | Your sphere renders in full 3D — you can fly right up to it and watch it rotate. |
| **Mecha progression** | Your mecha levels up with better engines, larger inventories, and combat capabilities (Dark Fog update). |

> 🧠 **Think of it as:** Factorio's production depth + Satisfactory's 3D world + the sheer cosmic ambition of building a star-encompassing power plant.

## 🪐 Planet Types & Resources

DSP's universe is procedurally generated, but every star cluster follows the same planet type archetypes. Understanding which planets to settle — and when — is critical.

### 🌱 Starting Planet (Temperate / Mediterranean)

Your home planet is always a **temperate world** with:

- **Iron, Copper, Stone, Coal** — abundant surface nodes
- **Oil** — crude oil seeps (unlocked by technology)
- **Water** — available via pumps on oceans or lakes
- **Wind and Solar** — decent efficiency, but not optimal
- **Titanium and Silicon** — *very limited* (you must expand to other planets for these)

> ⚠️ **Key Insight:** Your starting planet is designed to get you to blue science (matrix). Once you need yellow and purple science, you *must* leave your home planet. Do not over-invest in your starter base.

### 💎 Rare Ore Planets

Some planets in your cluster contain **rare ores** that dramatically simplify production chains. These are the ones worth colonising:

| Rare Ore | Found On | What It Replaces | Why It Matters |
|:---------|:---------|:-----------------|:--------------|
| **Kimberlite Ore** | Volcanic / Ash worlds | Replaces graphite chain for diamonds | Diamond is used in photonic processors and proliferator |
| **Optic Crystal** | Desert / Gobi worlds | Replaces processor chain | Bypasses the entire silicon-to-processor pipeline |
| **Spiniform Stalagmite** | Ice / Gelisol worlds | Replaces carbon nanotube chain | Direct source for nanotubes used in frames and science |
| **Fractal Silicon** | Barren / Desolate worlds | Replaces silicon smelting | Higher yield per node than standard silicon ore |
| **Grassstone** | Ocean / Swamp worlds | Replaces half of stone processing | High-volume stone substitute for concrete and bricks |

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** When you unlock **Interstellar Logistics Stations**, ship rare ores back to your main factory rather than building on-site. It's more energy-efficient to transport ore than to duplicate your production lines on every planet.

### 🌊 Ocean / Gas Giant Planets

Ocean planets and gas giants serve a different purpose:

- **Ocean Planets** — Vast water coverage. Excellent for **pump-based water extraction** and **offshore oil wells**. Some hold unique organic resources.
- **Gas Giants** — Cannot be landed on, but you can place **Orbital Collectors** in orbit to harvest hydrogen, deuterium, and fire ice. This is your primary source of hydrogen in the late game — far more efficient than cracking oil.
- **Ice Giants** — Same mechanic, but produce hydrogen and fire ice (critical for graphene and organic crystals).

| Resource | Best Source | Notes |
|:---------|:------------|:------|
| Hydrogen | Gas Giants (Orbital Collector) | 1 collector = ~15 oil refineries worth |
| Deuterium | Gas Giants or Particle Collider | Giant harvest is passive, collider is energy-intensive |
| Fire Ice | Ice Giants | Direct graphene source — skip the oil cracking |
| Crude Oil | Starting planet + Ocean worlds | You still need oil for plastic and sulphuric acid |

## 🚚 Logistics: Conveyer vs Drone vs Logistics Station

DSP offers three layers of logistics, each with a different role. Learning when to use each is the difference between a spaghetti nightmare and a clean, scalable factory.

### 🔹 Conveyer Belts (Planet-Level Transport)

- **Speeds:** Mk.I (6/s), Mk.II (12/s), Mk.III (30/s)
- **Range:** Unlimited, but limited to one planet
- **Best for:** Local factory connections, sorting, and precise throughput

**When to use belts:** Inside your factory blocks, for direct machine-to-machine connections, and for low-volume side products. Belts are zero-energy and fully predictable.

**When NOT to use belts:** Crossing long distances on a planet (use drones). Moving items between planets (use logistics stations). Organising late-game megabases at scale.

### 🔸 Logistics Drones (Planet-Level Distribution)

- **Range:** Limited to a single planet
- **Capacity:** Each drone carries a stack of items
- **Best for:** Moving items between different factory zones on the same planet

Drones require a **Planetary Logistics Station (PLS)** — a building that acts as a request/store node. Drones auto-balance supply and demand across PLS towers on the same planet.

**Pro tip:** One PLS with drones can replace *miles* of conveyor belt spaghetti. Use them liberally once you unlock them (around yellow science).

### 🔹 Interstellar Logistics Stations (ILS) (Star System–Level)

- **Range:** Unlimited (interstellar)
- **Capacity:** Vessels carry stacks across light-years
- **Fuel:** Requires warpers (green science) for interstellar travel
- **Best for:** Importing rare ores, exporting finished goods, creating a hub-and-spoke empire

The **Interstellar Logistics Station (ILS)** is the most important building in the game. Every megabase is built around a network of ILS towers.

| Feature | Conveyer Belt | Planetary Drone (PLS) | Interstellar Vessel (ILS) |
|:--------|:-------------|:----------------------|:--------------------------|
| **Range** | Same planet | Same planet | Any star system |
| **Energy** | Free | Requires tower power | Requires tower power + warpers |
| **Throughput** | Up to 30/s per belt | Limited by drone count | Limited by vessel count |
| **Best Use** | Factory subnetworks | Intra-planet distribution | Interplanetary supply chains |
| **Unlock** | Start | Yellow science | Purple science |

> 💡 **Golden rule:** Belts inside factories. Drones within a planet. ILS between planets. If you follow this, your factories will be clean and expandable.

## ☀️ Dyson Sphere Design

The core loop of DSP is building a Dyson sphere around a star to collect energy. Here's what you need to know.

### Solar Sails vs. Rockets

There are two ways to build your Dyson sphere, and they serve different purposes.

| Aspect | Solar Sails | Rockets |
|:-------|:------------|:--------|
| **What they build** | Transitory swarm → structural nodes | Permanent frame → solar panels |
| **Lifespan** | Temporary (degrade over time) | Permanent |
| **Power per unit** | Lower | Higher |
| **Resource cost** | Low (copper, silicon) | High (titanium, carbon nanotubes) |
| **Technology** | Blue science (early) | Purple science (mid-game) |
| **When to use** | Early-game power rush | Permanent sphere layers |

**Solar Sail Strategy:**
1. Build a **Dyson Swarm** first (free-floating sails around the star)
2. Sails degrade after ~2 hours, so they're a temporary bridge to rockets
3. Use sails to generate critical early power (ray receivers) while you research rockets
4. Once rockets unlock, transition to permanent sphere layers

**Rocket Strategy:**
1. Rockets build the **frame nodes** and **frame beams** of the sphere
2. After frames are placed, you can fill them with **Dyson panels** (also launched via rockets or sails)
3. A completed sphere layer generates *far* more power than any swarm
4. Multiple sphere layers can be built around the same star for multiplicative output

### Sphere Layer Tips

| Tip | Detail |
|:----|:-------|
| **Start with a small radius** | Begin at ~0.5–0.8 × the star's radius for your first layer. Small spheres complete faster and generate power sooner. |
| **Use the automatic editor** | The sphere editor lets you auto-generate structure layouts. Use the "triangle grid" preset for your first layer — it's the most efficient. |
| **Match receiver latitude** | Ray receivers on the poles of your planets have continuous line-of-sight to the sphere. Build them at high latitudes. |
| **Multiple layers = more power** | Each layer adds energy. Build 3–5 concentric layers around an O-type star for the best power density. |
| **Critical photon bottleneck** | Ray receivers convert sphere power to critical photons, which become antimatter for antimatter fuel rods and white science. Scale receivers *before* you need the fuel. |
| **Output depends on star type** | O-type and B-type stars output the most energy per node. Red dwarfs are weak. Build your sphere around the brightest star in your cluster. |

## ⭐ 5 Pro Tips

| # | Tip | Why It Matters |
|:-:|:----|:--------------|
| 1 | **Build a mall on your starting planet** | Automate production of belts, sorters, assemblers, smelters, and power poles before leaving your home planet. Hand-crafting buildings past blue science is a massive time sink. |
| 2 | **Use Planetary Logistics Stations as early as possible** | Unlock PLS at yellow science. Replace every long belt with a PLS pair (supplier + requester). This eliminates belt spaghetti and makes factory expansion infinitely easier. |
| 3 | **Ship raw ores, not intermediate products** | Ore stacks to 100. Processors stack to 20. Shipping raw ore via ILS is 5× more transport-efficient than shipping processed goods. Centralise your smelting and factory on one planet. |
| 4 | **Overbuild your power grid by 3×** | Every logistics tower, every ray receiver, and every production line chews power. Running out of power mid-expansion is devastating. Build 3× what you think you need, from wind/solar in early game to fusion or antimatter in late game. |
| 5 | **Never stop researching** | The research queue should never be empty. If you're bottlenecked on a science colour, scale up production of that colour before building anything else. White science (universe matrices) consumes all other sciences, so a balanced supply chain is critical. |

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Dyson Sphere Program multiplayer?**  
No. DSP is single-player only. The developer has stated they have no current plans for multiplayer, though mods can add co-op functionality.

**Can I build a Dyson sphere around any star?**  
Yes! Every star in your cluster can have a sphere. The output depends on the star type (O/B/A/F/G/K/M) — O-types give the most power.

**What is the "Dark Fog" update?**  
The Dark Fog update (2023) added enemy NPC factions that build bases on planets and harvest resources. You can fight them or coexist. It adds a combat dimension to a previously peaceful game.

**How long does it take to complete a Dyson sphere?**  
A first playthrough to launch a sphere takes roughly 60–100 hours. Optimised speedruns can do it in under 10 hours. The game is *big*.

**Are there mods?**  
Yes. The DSP modding community is active on Thunderstore. Popular mods include faster drones, custom sphere patterns, and quality-of-life improvements.
