## 🔧 Tool Details

### 🧮 Production Ratio Calculator

Coming soon for **Satisfactory**, **Factorio**, and **Dyson Sphere Program**.

Input a target item and production rate, and the calculator returns:

- **Machine counts** — exact number of smelters, constructors, assemblers, manufacturers, or assemblers needed
- **Belt throughput** — which belt tier handles the load, and whether splitters are balanced
- **Material requirements** — upstream consumption rates for every input ingredient
- **Byproduct handling** — for recipes that produce waste products (e.g. petroleum coke, uranium waste)

This tool will support *all base-game recipes* plus *alternate/hard-drive recipes* for Satisfactory and *all vanilla Factorio recipes* including modded content via JSON import.

### ⚡ Power Planner

Coming soon for **Satisfactory** and **Factorio**.

Stop guessing whether your coal plant is oversized. The Power Planner lets you:

- Model power plants by fuel type (coal, fuel, turbo fuel, nuclear, biomass, solar, wind)
- See exact fuel consumption per generator at 100% clock speed and underclocked
- Calculate backup power requirements for peak load
- Track byproduct fluids (water from coal generators, oil byproducts from refineries)
- Factor in overclocking and power shard usage for Satisfactory

For Factorio, it includes solar panel / accumulator ratios, nuclear reactor neighbour bonuses, and steam turbine throughput calculations.

### 🔧 Factory Planner

Coming soon — the **most requested tool** in our community feedback.

The Factory Planner is a full production chain visualiser. Start from the final product and work backward to raw resources. Key features:

- **Drag-and-drop** machine placement on a virtual grid
- **Real-time ratio updates** as you add or remove machines
- **Colour-coded resource flow** — spot bottlenecks instantly
- **Tier progression gate** — see which recipes are available at your current tech level
- **Export to CSV / image** — share your plan with teammates

Covers Satisfactory Tiers 0–9, Factorio vanilla through space science, and Dyson Sphere Program through green matrices.
