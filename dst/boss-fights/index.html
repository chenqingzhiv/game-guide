    DST Boss Database

    A comprehensive reference for every boss in Don't Starve Together, organized by category. Tables are DataTable-ready — sorting and search work out of the box.

    Seasonal Bosses

    These giants spawn on a seasonal timer unless you delay them with a Bearger / Deerclops effigy or the Celestial Portal.

    Deerclops — Winter Giant

    Stat Value
    HP 4,000
    Damage 75 (AoE, 60 AoE radius)
    Attack Period 3 seconds
    Insanity Aura -400 / minute
    Freezing Aura Yes (slow)
    Spawn Night of day 30+ (Winter), or first Winter day for new players
    Location Player vicinity

    Loot: 1 Deerclops Eyeball, 8 Meat, 1 Deerclops Fur

    Kite Strategy: Bait a swing → hit 3 times → back off. Repeat. Deerclops's AoE can destroy chests and structures — lure away from base.

    Recommended Gear: Ham Bat + Log Suit + Football Helmet. Eyebrella for rain immunity.

    Pro Tip: Deerclops destroys structures — fight far from base. The Eyebrella is the best rain hat for Spring.

    Bearger — Autumn Giant

    Stat Value
    HP 6,000
    Damage 100 (AoE ground pound)
    Attack Period 4 seconds
    Spawn Day 2–4 of Autumn (day 11+ on follow-up years)
    Location Player vicinity

    Loot: 8 Meat, 4 Bearger Fur, 1 Bearger Figure (Blueprint)

    Kite Strategy: Lure Bearger into a forest or pig village — he will destroy trees/pigs, dropping tons of wood and meat passively. When fighting directly: bait the ground pound (frontal AoE cone), run behind him, hit 4–5 times, repeat.

    Recommended Gear: Hambat + Marble Suit + Football Helmet. A grass suit works if kiting well.

    Pro Tip: Bearger's ground pound flings items from inventory. Drop anything fragile before engaging. Use him to clear a whole forest for free.

    Moose/Goose — Spring Giant

    Stat Value
    HP 4,000
    Damage 75 (peck) / 150 (charge)
    Attack Period 2.5 seconds
    Spawn First 5 days of Spring
    Location Near the mosaic biome / herding

    Loot: 4 Meat, 2 Down Feathers, 4 Moslings (if nest is left alone)

    Kite Strategy: Stay at range — Moslings (mini versions) spawn if you damage her. Use ranged weapons (boomerang, blow darts, fire staff). Kill Moslings first, then kite Moose/Goose between charges.

    Recommended Gear: Blow Darts / Fire Staff + Log Suit. A walking cane helps dodge charges.

    Pro Tip: Leave a nest alone for 4 days to get 4 Moslings — each drops 2 Down Feathers (used for Weather Pain, a top raid tool). Don't kill all Moslings or you lose the feather farm.

    Dragonfly — Summer Giant

    Stat Value
    HP 27,500
    Damage 75 (melee) / 300 (lava AoE)
    Attack Period 2 seconds
    Enrage Below 12,500 HP (faster, more AoE)
    Spawn Day 3–6 of Summer
    Location Dragonfly Desert

    Loot: 8 Meat, 1 Dragonfly Scale, 1–2 Gems (Red/Blue), 1 Lavae Egg (if pan flute used)

    Kite Strategy: Equip a pan flute — put Dragonfly to sleep immediately. Build a wall of endothermic fire pits to manage lavae. Kite horizontally: swing → dodge → repeat. Under 12,500 HP she enrages — use ice staves to freeze-lock.

    Recommended Gear: Thulecite Crown + Magiluminescence + Ice Staff. Weather Pain is highly effective.

    Pro Tip: Use the pan flute at the very start so you can build a containment arena. Dragonfly Scales craft the Scaled Furnace — infinite fuel, best cooking station in the game.

    Antlion — Summer Sand Giant

    Stat Value
    HP 2,500 (surface) / 2,500 (cave sinkhole)
    Damage 100 (melee) / 150 (sand spike AoE)
    Attack Period 3 seconds
    Spawn Day 3+ of Summer (if you pick up trinkets)
    Location Oasis Desert surface OR caves at sinkholes

    Loot: 8 Meat, 1 Antlion Mandible, 1 Desert Stone, Blueprint(s) for Desert Goggles / Sandstone

    Kite Strategy: Antlion's patterns are telegraph-heavy. Dodge sand spikes (3 waves), then close distance for 4 hits. On phase transitions, Antlion spawns sinkholes — run to dodge the falling sand AoE.

    Recommended Gear: Football Helmet + Log Suit + Ham Bat. A cobblestone road helps dodge sinkholes.

    Pro Tip: Ignore the summer surface version — it's optional. The cave Antlion protects sinkhole collapses. Feed Antlion trinkets (not Sammy) to delay sinkhole collapses.

    Raid Bosses

    Raids are multi-stage fights that require preparation: armor, healing, and arena setup. These are DST's hardest bosses.

    Bee Queen

    Stat Value
    HP 22,500
    Damage 60 (melee) / 80 (stinger)
    Attack Period 3 seconds
    Enrage Spawns Grumble Bees on HP thresholds
    Spawn Bee Queen Hive (Bee Biome) — needs hammer to spawn
    Location Bee Biome (usually in deciduous forest)

    Loot: 4–7 Honeycomb, 1 Bee Queen Crown, 1 Bundling Wrap BP, Royal Jelly, Gems

    Kite Strategy: Build a campfire wall arena — 4 endothermic fires spaced tight — and zigzag between them. Grumble Bees burn on contact. Bring a Weather Pain to clear Grumble waves.

    Recommended Gear: Thulecite Crown + Night Armor + Dark Sword. Healing salves / pierogi. A Star Caller's Staff heals Grumble Bees (doesn't help you) — avoid using it.

    Farming Tip: Bee Queen Crown + Magiluminescence + 4x lazy foragers = AFK honey farm. The crown is the best head-slot in the game for combat.

    Klaus

    Stat Value
    HP 10,000 (Klaus) / 2,500 (Red Gem Deer) / 2,500 (Blue Gem Deer)
    Damage 150 (melee) / varying (magic attacks with gems)
    Attack Period 3 seconds
    Spawn Klaus's Sack (random position in Deciduous forest, Winter)
    Location Deciduous Forest (Winter)

    Loot: 4–8 Meat, 1 Krampus Sack (rare), 1 Klaus's Sack, Gems, Ornate Chest items (bundles, lazy explorer, etc.)

    Kite Strategy: Kill the gem deer first — without them Klaus has no magic attacks. Focus the Red Gem Deer first (fire damage is more dangerous). Klaus's melee is tankable with good armor.

    Recommended Gear: Dark Sword + Thulecite Crown + Night Armor. Pan flute to separate the deer. Walking Cane helps dodge the magic AoE.

    Pro Tip: Klaus drops a Krampus Sack! Best chance of any source. Use a Deconstruction Staff on the sack at the end for extra loot.

    Toadstool

    Stat Value
    HP 52,500
    Damage 100 (melee) / poison spore AoE
    Attack Period 3 seconds
    Enrage At 50% HP — spawns boomshrooms & spore clouds
    Spawn Mushroom ring in the Caves
    Location Deep Caves (blue/red/green mushroom forest ring)

    Loot: 8 Meat, 1 Toadstool Cap, Old Bell (if no Ancient Guardian killed yet), or 1–2 Gems

    Kite Strategy: Use Weather Pain — it shreds Toadstool through spore clouds. Bring a lot (7+ and fling-o-matics to pre-plant). Fight in an open area so boomshrooms don't block you. When boomshrooms appear, weasel away and use gunpowder (20+) to clear the phase.

    Recommended Gear: Thulecite Crown + Night Armor + Dark Sword. 7+ Weather Pains. Gunpowder (optional). Heals: pierogi + honey poultice.

    Pro Tip: Toadstool has the highest base HP in DST. Bring a group or use the sleep + gunpowder strat (4 gunpowder = 5,000 damage).

    Ancient Guardian / Fuelweaver

    Stat Value
    HP 10,000 (Ancient Guardian) → 16,000 (Fuelweaver)
    Damage 100 (melee, Guardian) / 100 (woven attack, Fuelweaver)
    Attack Period 2.5 seconds
    Phase 2 (Fuelweaver) Weaves shadow hands, bone cage, heals on shadows
    Spawn Ancient Guardian: labyrinth → Fuelweaver: platform after Guardian
    Location Ruins (Ancient Gateway)

    Loot (FG): 4–6 Meat, Shadow Heart, Shadow Atrium, Bone Armor, Bone Helm, Fuelweaver Figure

    Kite Strategy (Ancient Guardian): Bait → hit 2–3 times → dodge. Guardian is straightforward. Save the Old Bell if dropped.

    Kite Strategy (Fuelweaver): The hardest fight in DST. You need an arena with Lureplants (distract shadows) and Weather Pains (clear bone cage while he's in it). Bait the bone cage → wait for his hand slam → dodge → hit his Woven Body 3–4 times. The fight is a rhythm game — don't get greedy.

    Recommended Gear (FG): Thulecite Crown x2 + Night Armor + Dark Sword. + 4+ Weather Pains + Lureplant bulbs. Healing: pierogi, honey poultice, jellybeans.

    Pro Tip: Fuelweaver's bone cage can be destroyed with a Weather Pain. His shadow hands are distracted by Lureplants. Never fight him in a cramped ruins corridor — clear an arena.

    Celestial Champion

    Stat Value
    HP 30,000 (total across 3 phases)
    Phase 1 10,000 HP — flying, projectile attacks
    Phase 2 10,000 HP — grounded, melee combos
    Phase 3 10,000 HP — empowered, all attacks
    Damage 100–200 (varies by phase and attack)
    Spawn Celestial Altar (Moon Island) — requires Celestial Tribute
    Location Lunar Island (Moon Quay biome)

    Loot: 4 Meat, Celestial Orb, Enlightened Crown Shard, Moon Rock, Gems

    Kite Strategy: Each phase has a different pattern. - Phase 1: Stay under the Champion (it can't hit you point-blank with projectiles). - Phase 2: Bait the spin attack, dodge, hit 3 times. Watch for the line-rush. - Phase 3: Combines both — focus on dodging. Enlightened Crown fragments drop and help with planar damage.

    Recommended Gear: Void Cowl + Brightshade Armor + Dark Sword (or Planar weapons). Brightshade Staff is excellent here.

    Pro Tip: Bring a Star Caller's Staff — it lights the arena and heals sanity during the fight. Celestial Champion is required for the rift endgame content.

    Mini-Bosses

    These are not seasonal or raid bosses, but pose significant threats and drop valuable loot.

    Spider Queen

    Stat Value
    HP 2,500
    Damage 80 (melee)
    Attack Period 2 seconds
    Spawn Level 3 Spider Den left alone too long
    Location Spider biomes / anywhere with spider nests

    Loot: 4 Meat, 2 Spider Eggs, 1 Spider Queen Silk, Glands, Silk

    Strategy: Fire Staff is the counter — one hit stuns her and burns the spiderlings. Kite: charge, hit 2–3 times, retreat. Clear her spawns with aoe (sleep darts + gunpowder optional).

    Pro Tip: A captive Spider Queen can be moved to a safe location and used as a renewable silk farm (if caged with walls).

    Treeguard

    Stat Value
    HP 5,000 (Living Log) / 7,500 (Totally Normal) / 10,000 (Spooky)
    Damage 100–150 (varies by size)
    Attack Period 3 seconds
    Spawn Chopping trees — chance increases with trees cut
    Location Forest biomes

    Loot: 3–6 Living Log, 1 Monster Meat (per size), Pine Cones

    Strategy: Plant pine cones nearby — Treeguards calm down when they see you replanting. If fighting: bait → hit 2–3 times → dodge. Fire deals extra damage to Treeguards.

    Pro Tip: Treeguards are renewable Living Log farms. Don't kill them — let them guard your forest and only fight when needed.

    Poison Birch (Birchnut Treeguard)

    Stat Value
    HP 3,000
    Damage 80 (melee) / poison effect
    Attack Period 2.5 seconds
    Spawn Chopping Birchnut Trees in Autumn
    Location Deciduous Forest

    Loot: 2–4 Birchnuts, Twigs, Logs

    Strategy: Bait → hit 2 times → retreat. Poison effect deals 2 damage/sec for 60 seconds — antivenom or waiting it out works. Use Log Suit to reduce damage.

    Pro Tip: Birchnut Treeguards only spawn in Autumn. They're much weaker than regular Treeguards.

    Krampus

    Stat Value
    HP 200
    Damage 100
    Attack Period 2 seconds
    Spawn When you kill innocent creatures (naughtiness > 30)
    Location Player's vicinity

    Loot: 1 Monster Meat, 1 Charcoal, 1 Krampus Sack (1% drop chance)

    Strategy: Krampus is fragile — 2–3 hits from a spear kills him. The challenge is making him spawn: kill birds, rabbits, or Pengulls to accumulate naughtiness. Wait for nighttime for the spawn.

    Pro Tip: Krampus Sack (14 inventory slots) is the most valuable item in DST. Farm during Winter nights with a bird trap + kill cycle for best odds.

    Malbatross

    Stat Value
    HP 3,000
    Damage 100 (melee) / wave splash
    Attack Period 2.5 seconds
    Spawn Ocean — near Malbatross nest / floating debris
    Location Open ocean (swimming)

    Loot: 4 Meat, 2–3 Malbatross Feathers, 1 Malbatross Bill

    Strategy: Boat fight — bring a Cookie Cutter-armored boat or Cookie Cutter Cap to reinforce. Kite: sail alongside, hit when she surfaces, dodge waves by sailing perpendicular. A second boat helps as a platform.

    Recommended Gear: Boat + Sail + Rudder + Anchor. Use a Boomerang or Blow Dart to aggro. Dark Sword + Log Suit.

    Pro Tip: Malbatross Bill + 2 rope + 1 twig = Malbatross Sail (105% boat speed, 5% wetness resistance upgrade) — the best sail in the game.

    Crab King

    Stat Value
    HP 10,000 (shell) + 12,500 (king)
    Damage 75–150 (claw slam, bubble, coral)
    Attack Period Variable (shell must be cracked first)
    Spawn Ocean — near the Moon Archipelago
    Location King Crab shell at sea (must be cracked at the Shell with gems)

    Loot: 8 Meat, Crab King Shell, Pearl Pearl, Gems, Blueprint(s)

    Strategy: Crab King requires gems inserted into the shell to crack it. The fight has 5 gem slots — the more gems, the better the loot. He spawns bubbles (insta-kill if trapped) and coral guards. Use a Weather Pain to clear coral. Boat must be armored.

    Recommended Gear: Boat with Armored Boat Kit + Cookie Cutter Cap + Dark Sword. Weather Pain. Heals: Jellybeans / pierogi.

    Pro Tip: Crab King drops the Crab King Shell — used to craft the Shell Amulet (planar defense). Bring Purple Gems for the shell to maximize loot quality.

    Armored Bearger

    Stat Value
    HP 7,500
    Damage 125
    Attack Period 3 seconds
    Spawn Post-rift (Rifts active) — replaces regular Bearger
    Location Same areas as Bearger

    Loot: 8 Meat, 4 Armored Bearger Fur, 1 Bearger Figure (BP)

    Strategy: Same kite pattern as Bearger, but higher damage and HP. Phase 2 adds a shockwave that throws items from inventory. Clear ground before engaging.

    Recommended Gear: Brightshade Sword + Void Cowl + Planar armor. Normal weapons do reduced planar damage — bring planar gear.

    Scrappy Werepig

    Stat Value
    HP 12,500
    Damage 100 (melee) / 150 (charge)
    Attack Period 2.5 seconds
    Spawn Rift event — Werepig transformation on full moon with rift active
    Location Pig King biome

    Loot: 8 Meat, 1 Scrappy Werepig Tail, 1 Pure Brilliance, Blueprint(s), Gems

    Strategy: Phase 1: regular Werepig kite pattern (bait → hit 3→ dodge). At 50% HP, goblin constructs spawn — kill them first. Phase 2: increased speed and charge attacks. Use Brightshade gear for planar damage.

    Recommended Gear: Brightshade Sword + Void Cowl + Planar armor. Healing: pierogi.

    Pro Tip: Scrappy Werepig drops Pure Brilliance, critical for rift-tier crafting. The tail crafts a weapon that debuffs planar entity defense.

    Frostjaw

    Stat Value
    HP 5,000
    Damage 100 (bite) / freezing breath AoE
    Attack Period 2.5 seconds
    Spawn Ocean — Winter only, near icebergs
    Location Frozen ocean biomes

    Loot: 4 Meat, 1 Frostjaw Tooth, 1 Frostjaw Horn, Gems

    Strategy: Boat fight with ice hazards. Frostjaw's freezing breath can freeze your boat — bring an Endothermic Fire onboard to thaw. Kite: sail alongside, hit when she surfaces, dodge freezing breath by sailing away.

    Recommended Gear: Boat + Sail + Endothermic Fire. Dark Sword. Thermal Stone for body temperature management.

    Pro Tip: Frostjaw Tooth crafts the Frostjaw Saddle (freeze-resistant saddle for Beefalo). The horn crafts a freezing weapon that works well in ocean combat.

    Boss Progression Order

    Recommended order for new players to tackle bosses in DST:

    1. Deerclops (Winter, first season) — easiest giant, teaches kiting
    2. Spider Queen (anytime) — teaches crowd control
    3. Moose/Goose (Spring) — introduces ranged combat
    4. Treeguard (anytime) — Living Logs for magic items
    5. Bearger (Autumn) — good loot, free wood farm
    6. Antlion (Summer) — teaches item bribery
    7. Dragonfly (Summer) — first real raid boss
    8. Klaus (Winter) — needs preparation and pan flute
    9. Ancient Guardian (Ruins) — unlocks caves progression
    10. Bee Queen (anytime with arena) — endgame gear upgrade
    11. Toadstool (Caves) — Weather Pain required
    12. Crab King (Ocean) — boat combat
    13. Malbatross (Ocean) — best sail in the game
    14. Fuelweaver (Ruins) — hardest fight in base DST
    15. Celestial Champion (Moon Island) — rift endgame entry
    16. Armored Bearger (Post-rift) — planar gear required
    17. Scrappy Werepig (Post-rift) — planar endgame
    18. Frostjaw (Winter, Ocean) — endgame ocean boss
