    Timberborn Update 7 — Bots, Water Physics Rework, and New Diorama Mode

    Timberborn Update 7 — The Bot Revolution

    Mechanistry has released Update 7 for Timberborn, the beaver colony city-builder. This is the largest update since the game's early access launch, introducing autonomous worker bots, a complete water physics engine overhaul that enables realistic river dynamics, and a Diorama Mode for creative builders. The update is available now on Steam and GOG.

    Bots — Automated Beaver Workforce

    The headline feature is a new automation layer: Bots. These are mechanical beavers that don't need sleep, food, or water, but require resources to build and maintain.

    Unlocking bots: Bots are unlocked after researching Advanced Mechanics at the District Center. Both the Folktails and Iron Teeth factions get bots, but their designs and requirements differ.

    Folktails Bots: - Scrap Bot — Cheaper to build (50 scrap, 10 planks, 5 gears). Lower stamina. Moves at 80% speed. Requires charging at a Bot Station every 3 days. - Clockwork Bot — Requires treated planks, gears, and a small gear mechanism. Faster (110% speed), higher stamina. Requires no charging but needs regular maintenance at a Workshop.

    Iron Teeth Bots: - Hydraulic Bot — Built with metal blocks, pipes, and treated planks. Runs on water pressure — must be connected to a water pipe network. Continuous operation if water supply is maintained. - Quantum Bot — Endgame bot. Requires quantum processors (crafted from metal blocks + gears + extract from a new Deep Mine resource). Virtually unlimited stamina, works at 120% speed. Very expensive per unit.

    Bot mechanics: - Bots work 24/7 with no breaks. - They can be assigned to any job in the priority system. - Bots do not reproduce — must be manually constructed. - Bots cannot work in specific buildings marked "Beaver-Only" (the Nursery, the Breeding Pod, and certain faction-unique buildings). - If a Bot runs out of charge or water pressure, it becomes inert and must be hauled to a repair station.

    Water Physics Engine Overhaul

    Update 7 replaces Timberborn's original water simulation with a new physics engine that dramatically changes how water flows through your colony.

    What changed:

    • River currents — Water now has directional flow speed. A sloped river channel moves water faster than a flat pool. Builders can use this to create run-of-river hydroelectric dams.
    • Sediment and erosion — Water running over dirt slowly erodes it (visual only, doesn't change terrain height). Water over hard surfaces (wood, metal, stone) does not erode.
    • Water pressure — Deep water exerts more pressure, which can break poorly-built dams. The new "Reinforced Dam" piece (unlocked at District Center) can hold back deep reservoirs.
    • Evaporation rate — Adjusted based on water surface area vs. volume. Wide shallow ponds evaporate faster than deep narrow channels.
    • Seasonal water levels — A new map option called "Seasonal River" makes water levels fluctuate. Spring rains raise the river, summer drought lowers it. Bep's (standard) maps also get a mild seasonal effect.

    Visual updates: Water now has: - Foam lines at river edges and around obstacles - Depth-based color gradient (shallow = clear green, deep = dark blue) - Surface ripple variance based on flow speed

    Diorama Mode

    A brand-new creative mode for players who love building without survival pressure:

    Diorama Mode features: - Unlimited resources — All buildings, decorations, and terrain tools are unlocked from the start. - God tools — Paint terrain, raise/lower land, place water sources, and spawn trees. - Time freeze — Pause time permanently while you build. - Screenshot tools — Free camera with focal length, aperture, and time-of-day controls for cinematic screenshots. - Save dioramas as .diorama files to share on the Steam Workshop.

    Other Changes

    • New building: Deep Mine — Required for high-tier resources (quantum ore, deep metal). Generates heat that must be ventilated.
    • New building: Hydro Turbine — Generates power from flowing water. Works best in fast-moving channels.
    • New decoration set — 15 ornamental pieces including topiary, statues, flagstone paths, and flower boxes.
    • Map updates — 3 new official maps (Canyon River, The Delta, Frosted Basin).
    • UI overhaul — Resource overlay now shows net production/consumption. Dams and levees have a health bar.

    Tips for Update 7

    1. Start with Folktails Scrap Bots — They're cheap and teach you the bot maintenance loop without heavy resource commitment.
    2. Reinforce your dams — Old dams may fail under the new water pressure system. Upgrade to Reinforced Dams before the wet season.
    3. Build on elevated ground — Seasonal water level fluctuations can flood low-lying districts in spring.
    4. Place Hydro Turbines in natural flow channels — Check the water overlay to find high-velocity areas.
