Satisfactory

Explore a lush alien world, build massive factories from first-person perspective, and optimize your production lines to create the ultimate automated empire.

9 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • First-person factory building — walk inside your own factory and see every machine up close, unlike traditional top-down builders
  • Massive 3D world — explore 7+ biomes including grasslands, deserts, jungles, and red bamboo forests, each with unique resources
  • Tier-based progression — 9 tiers with hundreds of unlocks from basic handcrafting to nuclear power and particle accelerators
  • Multiplayer co-op — up to 4 players can build together in real-time, with shared progression
  • Vehicles & transport — build trucks, tractors, trains, drones, and hypertube cannons for moving resources and yourself
  • Complete 1.0 release — full story mode with tier 9, new alien tech, and endgame goals

💡 Pro Tips

  • Build vertically! — Satisfactory encourages stacking. Use multiple floors for production lines instead of spreading horizontally across the map
  • Use foundations early — snapping buildings to foundations gives perfectly aligned factories. Never build on bare terrain
  • Overclock wisely — power slugs let you overclock miners first (240%-300% output per machine saves space), then production buildings
  • Plan your main bus — leave 2-3 foundation gaps between production rows for future belt upgrades (Mk.1 → Mk.5 belts)
  • Drones are endgame heroes — once you unlock drones, use them to ship low-volume high-value items (computers, supercomputers) across the map

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Start near a pure iron node — iron plates, rods, and screws are the foundation of everything
  • Handcraft only the first few items; automate iron plates and rods as soon as you unlock the constructor
  • Build a biomass burner farm early — 8-10 burners with a dedicated biofuel production line keep you running until coal power
  • Explore crash sites for hard drives — they unlock alternate recipes that can dramatically simplify production
  • Don't tear down your starter base — build a new one nearby instead. Your first factory still produces useful parts for building

Factorio

The definitive factory automation game. Mine resources, research technologies, build ever-expanding factories, and defend against native lifeforms.

7 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Infinite scalability — grow from a single burner miner to a megabase covering thousands of tiles with millions of items per minute
  • Complex logistics — design rail networks, bot swarms, and belt highways to move resources across your factory
  • Circuit network — build programmable logic controllers with combinators for fully automated train schedules, oil cracking, and defense systems
  • Massive modding community — thousands of mods from total overhauls (Space Exploration, Krastorio 2, Seablock) to quality-of-life tweaks
  • Space Age expansion — the 2024 expansion introduces interplanetary logistics, new planets with unique resources, and end-game space platforms
  • Enemy evolution — biters evolve as you pollute, forcing you to balance factory growth with military defense

💡 Pro Tips

  • The 80/20 rule — 80% of your base will be making green circuits, red circuits, and plastic. Perfect these three and everything else falls into place
  • Main bus design — a 4-lane main bus (iron, copper, steel, circuits) with 2 empty lanes between each keeps your early-mid game organized
  • Use trains early — as soon as you unlock rail, build a 2-track rail network. Doubling back later is painful
  • Solar is not free — solar panels have zero running cost but massive upfront material cost. Use them only for remote outposts; use nuclear for your main base
  • Ratio cheat sheet — 1 pumpjack : 10 refineries for basic oil; 1 iron gear wheel assembler feeds 10 red science assemblers; 1 copper wire assembler feeds 6 green circuit assemblers

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Start with a simple goal: automate red science. Just iron plates + copper plates → gear wheels → red science
  • Leave more space than you think you need. Factorio's motto: "The factory must grow"
  • Use the map view (M) to plan your base layout before building
  • Build a wall and turrets around your base before pollution reaches the first biter nest
  • Wood is terrible fuel. Switch to coal as soon as possible for your furnaces and boilers
  • The Alt-mode (Alt key) shows what each building is producing — keep it on at all times

Dyson Sphere Program

Harness the power of entire star systems. Build a Dyson Sphere across multiple planets while managing interplanetary logistics at cosmic scale.

5 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Multi-planet logistics — manage resources across entire star systems with interplanetary transporters and logistics stations
  • Build actual Dyson Spheres — construct visible Dyson spheres with solar sails, frame nodes, and energy receivers that you can see from a planet's surface
  • Interstellar travel — explore multiple star systems with unique planet types: desert, ocean, lava, ice, and organic worlds
  • Stunning cosmic scale — start with conveyor belts on one planet, end with a complete Dyson Swarm harvesting the energy of an entire star
  • Research tech tree — hundreds of technologies from basic automation to matter conversion and gravitational manipulation
  • Combat update (Rise of the Dark Fog) — optional enemy faction adds strategic tower defense and salvage mechanics

💡 Pro Tips

  • Build on the equator — placing your factory along the equator gives the longest day-night cycle for solar and consistent belt throughput
  • Logistics station spacing — leave room between ILS (Interstellar Logistics Stations) for drone upgrades. You'll thank yourself later
  • Sphere before warpers — your first Dyson Sphere should be a simple ring, not a full sphere. Use it to generate photons for warper production
  • Rush green science — green science unlocks logistics towers, which are the key to scalable production
  • Smelt on mining planets — ship ingots instead of raw ore to save 50-70% on interplanetary transport capacity

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Your starting planet always has the best conditions for building. Don't rush to leave it
  • Build more wind turbines than you think you need — wind power is consistent and works at night
  • Use Sorters with the correct tier — use fastest sorters for ore, slower ones for intermediate products
  • Set up a dedicated matrix (science) production line early and feed it by logistics towers
  • Tier 1 conveyor belts move 6 items/sec; Tier 3 moves 30 items/sec. Upgrade belts rather than adding more parallel lines
  • Save often and keep old saves — one mistake can grind your entire production to a halt

Timberborn

Control a colony of beavers in a post-human world. Manage resources, build dams, and survive droughts in this unique colony sim.

7 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Unique vertical building — build stackable structures that can reach impressive heights. Your beaver colony grows upward, not outward
  • Dynamic water physics — build dams, canals, levees, and floodgates to control real-time water flow. Droughts and floods are your primary challenge
  • Two beaver factions — Folktails (nature-friendly, farming-focused) vs Iron Teeth (tech-focused, industrialized). Completely different buildings and strategies
  • Seasonal drought cycles — survive through droughts, manage water reservoirs, and thrive when water returns
  • District system — divide your colony into districts connected by distribution routes for efficient resource management across large maps
  • Go, explore! mode — procedurally generated maps with adjustable difficulty, map size, and water settings for infinite replayability

💡 Pro Tips

  • Build your reservoir day one — before your first drought, build a 3-block-deep reservoir with floodgates. A single map edge water source can sustain 200+ beavers
  • Use platforms for density — platforms let you build on top of buildings. Stack your industry vertically to minimize walking distance
  • Folktails: go farming — with Folktails, focus on irrigation and farmland. Their Iron Teeth counterparts rely heavily on water wheels for power
  • Power management — build water wheels in series along your dam's overflow. One 3-wide channel with 10 wheels generates more power than 2 separate channels
  • District distribution — dedicate one district to food and wood, another to industry. Use distribution towers to ship goods between them

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Start with the Folktails faction — they're more forgiving with simpler food production
  • Water is life: always build near a water source. You cannot expand without a reliable water supply
  • Build a forester's lodge early and plant trees around your colony. Wood is used for everything
  • Keep an eye on your beaver population. Each beaver consumes 1 food/day and needs a bed
  • Don't overbuild housing — beavers breed to fill available beds. Keep breeding under control
  • Pause buildings during droughts: clicking the pause button on a building saves resources and power

Shapez 2

A pure logistics and automation puzzle. No enemies, no combat — just shapes, belts, and the satisfaction of an perfectly optimized factory floor.

6 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Pure logistics puzzle — no enemies, no combat, no RNG, no time pressure. Just you and the satisfaction of perfect automation
  • Shape-based production — produce, cut, rotate, combine, and paint shapes with infinite combinatorial possibilities
  • Blueprint system — design reusable factory blueprints (called "stamps") and share them with other players
  • Color mixing mechanics — mix primary colors to create every color in the rainbow using color mixing machines
  • 3D upgrade from Shapez 1 — now with a third dimension for stacking belts and compact factory designs
  • Milestone-driven progression — each milestone unlocks new buildings and shapes, naturally guiding you to ever more complex factories

💡 Pro Tips

  • Build modular — design small, self-contained factories for each shape, then stamp them as blueprints. A library of 5-6 blueprints is enough to beat the game
  • Use color wheel logic — learn the color mixing rules: R+Y=O, R+B=P, Y+B=G, R+G=Y... proper color sequencing saves 50% of your machines
  • Cuter before painter — always cut shapes before painting them. Cutting after painting means you waste painted shapes that get cut away
  • Stack belts for density — use stackers to place belts on top of each other. A 4-layer belt stack carries 4x as much in the same footprint
  • Don't overproduce — unlike other factory games, each milestone only needs 10-100 of a shape. Overbuilding is a waste of space

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Complete the tutorial! It teaches you the core mechanics in 15 minutes
  • Start simple: produce one shape at a time. Each new milestone only needs one new target shape
  • Use the map view to plan your factory layout before building anything
  • Pause is your friend — the game pauses instantly with Escape. Use it to plan complex layouts
  • Every building has a dedicated color indicator — check the tiny color dot on buildings and belt splitters
  • Delete with the bucket tool — hold Shift and drag to delete large areas of belts and buildings instantly

Valheim

A brutal Viking survival adventure. Explore a purgatory-like world, defeat mythical bosses, build grand halls, and sail the seas of the Tenth World.

8 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Massive procedurally-generated world — Valheim generates a unique world every time with millions of square kilometers of explorable terrain across several biomes
  • Five biome bosses — Eikthyr (Meadows), The Elder (Black Forest), Bonemass (Swamp), Moder (Mountains), Yagluth (Plains) — each with unique mechanics and loot
  • Advanced building system — structural integrity forces realistic building. A stone roof needs proper support, or it collapses. The most creative building system in survival games
  • Co-op for up to 10 players — dedicated servers, shared progression, and a community that players call the most friendly in survival gaming
  • Sailing and naval combat — build rafts, karves, and longships to explore distant lands. Battles at sea with serpents add epic adventure
  • Bog Witch update — the latest major update adds new potions, cooking recipes, the Marsh Hut merchant, and quality-of-life improvements

💡 Pro Tips

  • Food is your real health bar — in Valheim, your health pool depends entirely on what you've eaten. Always carry 3 different food types (health, stamina, mixed)
  • Upgrade your gear before boss fights — each boss is a tier check. If Bonemass (Swamp boss) is crushing you, upgrade your iron gear and poison resist mead
  • Build a portal network — portals are cheap (10 fine wood, 2 surtling cores, 10 greydwarf eyes). Place named portals at every major base and exploration point
  • Parry, don't block — learning to parry (blocking right as an attack lands) staggers enemies and opens them to 3x damage. It's the most important combat skill
  • Use the hoe everywhere — the hoe lets you level ground. Use it to flatten terrain around your base, build roads, and create moats against raids

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Don't rush! Valheim is about the journey. Build a proper base in the Meadows before venturing into the Black Forest
  • Always repair your gear at the workbench. A broken weapon deals 75% less damage
  • Build a shelter your first night — a simple 4-wall hut with a roof, bed, campfire, and workbench is enough to survive
  • Set your spawn point by sleeping in a bed (must be a sheltered bed with fire nearby)
  • Collect all the raspberries, blueberries, and mushrooms you find — cooking ingredients are critical for food buffs
  • Avoid the Plains biome until you have at least iron gear. Deathsquitos will one-shot a leather-clad Viking

Enshrouded

Rise from the ashes in a vast fantasy wasteland. Battle through the Shroud, build your stronghold, and uncover the secrets of a fallen kingdom.

6 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Voxel-based building — full terraforming with the ability to dig into mountains, build underground bases, and shape terrain to your will
  • The Shroud mechanic — a deadly magical fog covers parts of the map. Venturing into the Shroud requires special Shroud survival time, adding risk-reward exploration
  • Class-less skill tree — no class restrictions. Mix and match skills from warrior, ranger, mage, and healer trees for unique hybrid builds
  • Up to 16-player co-op — massive multiplayer sessions with shared building projects, dedicated server support, and crossplay
  • Dynamic crafting — utilize the Flame Altar upgrade system to expand your building radius and craft increasingly powerful gear
  • Horizons update — the latest major update adds the new Pyre biome, flying mounts, new enemies, weapons, and expanded building pieces

💡 Pro Tips

  • Upgrade your Flame Altar first — every Flame Altar level increases your building radius and unlocks crafting stations. Prioritize upgrading it before anything else
  • Build a shrine near Shroud roots — each Shroud root you destroy adds a permanent buff. Place a spawn point nearby so you can keep pushing the objective
  • Hybrid mage builds are OP — the most powerful builds combine magic (for AoE) with melee (for single-target). A war-mage with staff + sword is devastating
  • Use NPC summoning — rescue craftspeople and workers scattered across the map. They unlock permanent crafting recipes and bonuses for your base
  • Glide everywhere — unlock the glider as soon as possible. It dramatically speeds up exploration and lets you reach high-placed Shroud roots

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Build your first base near the first Flame Altar location — you'll return here often
  • Don't be afraid of the Shroud — early zones have low-damage Shroud. Learn its mechanics early
  • Craft the grappling hook as soon as you can — it's essential for exploration and reaching high areas
  • Save all your Shroud liquids — they're used for advanced crafting and potions later
  • Build a dedicated crafting hall early. You'll need space for 10+ crafting stations as you progress
  • Rescue the Hunter NPC first — she unlocks the best early-game ranged weapons for safe combat

V Rising

Awaken as a weakened vampire. Hunt for blood, build a dark castle, forge alliances, and conquer the world of Vardoran in this gothic survival RPG.

7 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Blood system — different blood types (brute, creature, warrior, rogue, scholar) give different buffs. Mixing blood qualities changes your combat effectiveness
  • Castle building with gothic architecture — build a vampire castle complete with coffins, gardens, servants' quarters, and throne rooms using hundreds of decorative pieces
  • V-Blood boss progression — defeat 50+ V-Blood carriers, each with unique abilities. Defeating them unlocks new forms, spells, and crafting recipes
  • PvP and PvE servers — choose from full loot PvP, raid weekends, or pure PvE. The game adapts to your preferred playstyle
  • Form-switching combat — transform into a wolf (stealth), bat (travel), or human (social interaction). Each form has unique gameplay benefits
  • Full 1.0 launch — the complete game includes the Ruins of Mortium zone, Dracula boss fight, and the ending of the vampire saga

💡 Pro Tips

  • 100% blood is the goal — always try to feed on enemies with 90-100% blood quality. The difference between 50% and 100% is roughly 2x combat power
  • Build near a fishing pond or crossroads — your castle's location determines what resources are nearby. A central location saves hours of travel time
  • Gardens are underrated — plant ghost shrooms and fire blossoms for potions. A dedicated 12-plot garden sustains all basic alchemy
  • Servants are loot farms — send servants on missions to gather end-game materials while you focus on progression. Train them up for better results
  • Castle heart upgrade priority — level 2 castle heart unlocks gardens, level 3 unlocks servants. Rush these two upgrades

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Stay out of the sun! Daylight damages you — build a covered route from your castle to nearby points of interest
  • Feed on low-level enemies first to practice combat without risk
  • Unlock the bat form as soon as possible — it's your primary fast-travel ability
  • Build a coffin and set your respawn point at your castle immediately
  • Defeat Keely the Frost Archer early — she drops the recipe for the best early-game weapon
  • Edge-of-map locations are safest for castle building — fewer players finding you in PvP

Sons of the Forest

Stranded on a remote island after a helicopter crash. Build shelter, craft weapons, and survive against cannibalistic mutants in this terrifying open-world horror.

4 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Horror-themed open-world survival — a dark, atmospheric island with day/night cycles, dynamic weather, and terrifying cannibal camps
  • AI companion Kelvin — your deaf survival companion follows verbal commands (using the notebook). He can chop wood, fish, build, and carry items for you
  • Dynamic building system — construct bases using real-time structural physics. Chop down trees and watch the logs fall realistically
  • Cave systems with story — Indiana Jones-style exploration with 10+ cave systems containing lore, loot, and terrifying creature encounters
  • Co-op campaign — full 8-player co-op with shared story progression. Friends can join and leave at any time
  • Seasons and weather — dynamic seasons affect gameplay. Winter brings snow and cold survival challenges; summer is easier but attracts more cannibal activity

💡 Pro Tips

  • Kelvin is your best weapon — assign Kelvin to build log storage and defensive walls while you explore. He's slow but never stops working
  • Build on the beach — building near the water gives you one open attack direction, making defense significantly easier. Cannibals can't swim well
  • Use the GPS tracker — mark caves, loot spots, and water sources on your GPS map. The island is massive and easy to get lost in
  • Headshots with the crafted bow — the basic crafted bow one-shots most cannibals with a headshot. It's silent, cheap, and the best early weapon
  • Armor stacks help — you can wear multiple armor types simultaneously. Creepy armor + leaf armor + bone armor = dramatic damage reduction

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Build a small shelter before night 1 — cannibals are much more aggressive at night
  • Find the 3D printer in a cave early — it lets you craft essential items for 50 printer resin each
  • Collect all the duct tape you find — it's needed for almost every advanced craft
  • Turn on Kelvin's "carry items" mode immediately — he's a mobile inventory extension
  • Don't fight on your first day — explore, gather, build. Combat becomes much easier with proper gear
  • Listen for the creepy music — it means a major threat is nearby. Be ready or run

Grounded

Shrunk to the size of an ant. Explore the dangers of a suburban backyard — from towering grass blades to fearsome spiders — in this cooperative survival adventure.

3 guides available

🎯 Key Features

  • Unique micro-scale backyard world — everything is enormous from your perspective. A single dew drop is a water source; a garden ant is a fearsome enemy
  • Base building on giant objects — build your base on a basketball, inside a shoe, or under a garden lamp. Every object is a potential building site
  • Creature-filled ecosystem — fight ants, spiders, beetles, mosquitoes, and lawnmites. Creatures have territorial behaviors and day/night cycles
  • Full 4-player co-op campaign — complete story from start to finish with friends. Cross-platform multiplayer (Steam, Xbox, Game Pass)
  • Tiered weapon and armor system — progress from pebblet daggers and acorn armor to mint maces, antlion swords, and roly poly armor
  • Mystery and exploration — uncover what happened to Dr. Wendell Tully through lab notes, audio logs, and hidden backyard secrets

💡 Pro Tips

  • Build near a field station — field stations let you analyze resources and unlock new recipes. Building near one saves constant back-and-forth trips
  • Use the Pebblet Turkey for early water — the Pebblet Turkey (small upside-down container near the starting area) collects dew drops. You'll never lack water
  • Dandelion tufts are your parachute — a dandelion tuft in your off-hand lets you glide down safely from any height. Essential for exploring the oak tree
  • Perfect block timing — blocking right before an attack lands negates all damage. Practice perfect blocks on ants before taking on spiders
  • Spider armor is a trap — ladybug armor with the sleek upgrade path is better than spider armor for general exploration due to healing bonuses

🆕 Beginner Tips

  • Analyze everything at the field station — pebbles, plant fiber, sap, clover. Each analysis unlocks new recipes
  • Build a lean-to and set your respawn point immediately — death is frequent in the early game
  • Use the peaked roof (slanted roof) for water collection — rain runs off angled roofs into a small pool at the base
  • Avoid wolf spiders at night — they roam the map and are terrifying for new players. They sleep in the oak tree during the day
  • Don't build on or near ant hills — ants will eat your food storage and can destroy your base
  • Craft the bug slapper (stun weapon) from a bombardier beetle part — it's a game-changer for crowd control
