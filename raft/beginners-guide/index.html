    Raft Beginners Guide

    Raft drops you on a 2x2 wooden platform in the middle of an endless ocean. With nothing but a plastic hook, you must gather debris, expand your raft, and survive sharks, hunger, and thirst. This guide covers everything from your first 10 minutes to reaching the final story island.

    First 10 Minutes: From 2x2 to 4x4

    Immediate Priority

    1. Hook everything — Use your default plastic hook to grab every piece of floating debris. Nothing is useless.
    2. Prioritize these resources first:
    Resource Minimum Needed Used For
    Plastic 15 Hook upgrade, purifier, collection nets
    Wood 20 Foundation, walls, tools, cooking
    Palm Leaf 10 Roof, sails, bed
    Scrap 5 Hook upgrade, tools, smelter
    Rope 3 (craft from Palm Leaf) Tools, anchor

    First Crafting Order

    Step Item Materials Why
    1 Plastic Hook 10 Plastic, 2 Rope Catch debris 3x faster
    2 Simple Purifier 5 Plastic, 2 Scrap, 1 Rope Boil seawater into drinking water
    3 Research Table 8 Plastic, 4 Scrap, 2 Rope Unlocks advanced recipes
    4 Collection Net 8 Plastic, 4 Rope, 2 Scrap Auto-collects floating debris
    5 Fishing Rod 5 Wood, 3 Rope Reliable food source
    6 Grill 4 Scrap, 4 Wood Cook fish and potatoes
    7 Smelter 4 Scrap, 4 Dry Brick, 6 Wood Smelt metal ore

    Raft Expansion Priority

    Raft Size Materials Benefit
    2x2 (starter) None Barely functional
    4x4 12 Foundation, 12 Collection Nets Room for all basics
    5x6 30 Foundation + nets Dedicated rooms for crafting, cooking, storage
    7x8 56 Foundation Full base with sails, anchor, engine room
    10x10+ 100+ Foundation Mega-raft with multiple floors

    Tip: Always expand your raft in the direction of the wind (the direction debris floats toward you). Collection nets on the windward side catch everything automatically.

    Resource Management

    Food & Water Systems

    Need Depletion Rate Danger Level
    Water Drops ~5% per 3 minutes Death in ~60 minutes at 0%
    Food Drops ~4% per 3 minutes Death in ~75 minutes at 0%
    Health Regens 1 HP/min if fed and hydrated Full regen in ~7 minutes

    Water Sources

    Source Water Yield How to Get
    Simple Purifier 1 Cup (~25%) 3x Seawater + 1 Plank fuel
    Advanced Purifier 2 Cups (~40%) 5x Seawater + 1 Scrap fuel (better efficiency)
    Coconuts ~8% water + food Found on tropical islands
    Rain (with cup equipped) Passive Stand in rain with cup, fills slowly

    Water Tip: Build 2+ Simple Purifiers early. Running out of water is the #1 cause of death for new players.

    Food Sources

    Food Hunger Restored Water Restored Preparation
    Raw Potato 8 0 Eat raw (inefficient)
    Baked Potato 18 0 Cook on grill
    Raw Beet 5 0 Eat raw
    Cooked Beet 14 0 Cook on grill
    Raw Fish 5 0 Eat raw
    Cooked Fish 25 0 Cook on grill
    Raw Shark Meat 10 0 Can't eat raw (food poisoning)
    Cooked Shark Meat 35 0 Cook on grill
    Coconut (drank + ate) 5 ~8 Consume both parts
    Drumstick (from birds) 7 0 Cook on grill
    Cooked Drumstick 20 0 Cook on grill
    Pot of Soup 50+ 20+ Cooking pot + vegetables

    Tip: Cooked fish is your best early food source. A fishing rod catches fish in 5–15 seconds. Cooked fish restores 25 hunger — 3 fish keep you fed for a whole day.

    Essential Upgrades in Order

    Phase 1: Survival (Day 1–3)

    Upgrade Why It's Essential
    Plastic Hook 3x faster debris collection
    Simple Purifier x2 Infinite water supply
    Collection Net x4 Auto-collect while you build
    Research Table Unlocks all recipes
    Grill + Smelter Food and metal processing

    Phase 2: Navigation (Day 3–7)

    Upgrade Materials Purpose
    Receiver 2 Circuit Board, 5 Metal Ingot, 1 Hinge Find story islands
    Antenna x3 1 Circuit Board, 4 Scrap, 1 Metal Ingot each Required for Receiver to work
    Steering Wheel 1 Circuit Board, 1 Hinge, 8 Plank Steer the raft
    Engine 6 Metal Ingot, 2 Hinge, 3 Circuit Board Move against wind
    Fuel Tank 4 Metal Ingot, 6 Plank, 1 Hinge Store engine fuel
    Large Storage x4 12 Plank, 12 Rope Organize resources

    Receiver Setup: Place the Receiver on the second floor or higher. Place 3 Antennas on the same floor, spaced 4+ blocks apart and with direct sky access (no roof over them).

    Phase 3: Fortification (Day 7+)

    Upgrade Purpose
    Reinforced Foundation Prevents Bruce (shark) from breaking your raft
    Metal Walls + Roof Protection from birds and waves
    Paint + Flags Personalization, landmark visibility
    Zipline Quick travel across mega-rafts

    Bruce: The Shark

    Bruce is your constant companion — a great white shark that circles your raft and attacks foundations.

    Shark Behavior

    Trigger Response
    Player in water Bites for 40 damage in 2 seconds
    Foundation hit Destroys 4 hits on wood (1 hit per attack)
    Metal foundation Ignores (can't damage)
    Shark bait in water Attacks bait for 30 seconds

    Dealing with Bruce

    Strategy Difficulty Effectiveness
    Ignore Trivial Low — he'll destroy all wooden foundations
    Repair Easy Medium — repair with hammer between attacks
    Reinforced foundations Medium Very High — Bruce can't damage them
    Kill Bruce Medium High — respawns in 2 minutes, gives 2 Shark Meat
    Shark bait Easy Low — only distracts for 30 seconds

    Pro Tip: Once you have scrap and metal, upgrade all outer foundations to Reinforced. Bruce will never break them, saving you endless repairs.

    Island Exploration

    Tropical Small Islands

    Resource Where to Find
    Palm Tree Beach (chop for wood + palm leaves)
    Pineapple Ground level, tropical plants
    Watermelon Ground level, tropical plants
    Beet Seeds Underground (shovel required)
    Potato Seeds Underground (shovel required)
    Clay Beach, underwater
    Sand Beach
    Metal Ore Underground, rocks
    Copper Ore Underground, deeper caves
    Scrap Beach, underwater wrecks
    Rope (nests) Treetops, bird nests

    Exploration Checklist: - [ ] Bring water (2+ cups) - [ ] Bring food (3+ cooked fish) - [ ] Bring a spear (for boar defense) - [ ] Bring a shovel (dig seeds and clay) - [ ] Bring a hook (grab underwater scrap) - [ ] Loot all crates (maps, notes, headpieces) - [ ] Check all 4 sides of the island

    Danger: Boars on large tropical islands charge you. They deal 30 damage. Use the spear and keep distance.

    Story Island Locations

    1. Radio Tower (First Story Island)

    Requirements: Receiver + 3 Antennas Receiving Frequency: Default (start of game) Objective: Climb radio tower, find notes, learn about the world

    Room Loot Danger
    Ground floor Food, water, notes None
    Mid floors Scrap, metal ore Looters (hostile NPCs)
    Top floor Blueprint: Headpiece, Journal pages None (climb with care)
    Rooftop Zip line to the top Missing platform (jump required)

    2. Vasagatan (Ship Wreck)

    Receiving Frequency: 4141 Objective: Navigate the ship, solve puzzles, reach the bridge

    Floor Puzzle/Challenge Loot
    Hull Locked doors (crowbar required) Food, scrap
    Lower deck Rats, poisonous water Blueprint: Metal Detector
    Mid deck Hostile NPCs Ammo, weapons
    Bridge Final puzzle Blueprint: Engine, Fuel Tank

    3. Caravan Island

    Receiving Frequency: 5252 Objective: Explore the mountain village, find the zipline network

    Area Challenge Loot
    Lower village Looters with guns Ammo, food
    Mid mountain Climbing puzzle (ropes) Blueprint: Zipline
    Summit Secret cave Rare items, notes

    4. Balboa Island

    Receiving Frequency: 6363 Objective: Survive the bear-infested island, find lore about the research station

    Area Danger Loot
    Forest Bears (highest damage enemy) Honey, berries
    Cave Poison spiders Blueprint: Headlight, fur
    Research station Puzzles Blueprint: Advanced Purifier

    5. Tangaroa (Underwater City)

    Receiving Frequency: 7474 Objective: Explore the flooded city, solve water puzzles, collect lore

    Area Challenge Loot
    Streets Flooded, oxygen timer Scrap, food
    Buildings Locked doors, keycards Blueprint: Fuel Pipe
    Control room Water level puzzle Final story reveal

    Receiving Frequency: Complete all previous islands Objective: Endgame area, final confrontation

    10 Beginner Tips

    1. Always anchor when exploring — Without an anchor, your raft drifts away while you're on an island. Craft an anchor immediately.
    2. Aim for the shark's nose — When Bruce attacks, wait until he opens his mouth (the lunge animation), then swing. This parries the attack.
    3. Build a water tank — Rain fills water tanks. One full tank holds 10 cups of water (enough for 30 minutes).
    4. Use the cooking pot — A pot of soup feeds you for 5+ servings. Much more efficient than grilling individual fish.
    5. Collect every scrap — Scrap is the bottleneck resource for mid-game upgrades. Never leave floating scrap.
    6. Head pieces mark story progression — Each story island gives a head piece (cosmetic hat). Collect all 6 for the achievement.
    7. Grow crops on your raft — Potatoes and beets can be planted in crop plots. 6 crop plots sustain 1 player indefinitely.
    8. Birds drop feathers — Shooting seagulls gives feathers for arrows. Arrows are infinite (recoverable from bodies).
    9. Paint your raft — Paint helps you spot your raft from a distance. Also lets you color-code storage.
    10. Save before story islands — Story islands have no return teleport. If you die on an island, your items are there until you go back. Bring a backup raft.
