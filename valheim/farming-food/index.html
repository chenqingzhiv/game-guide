    🌾 Valheim Farming & Cooking Guide

    Last updated: July 2026 | Game version: 0.219.14+

    Food is the single most important factor in Valheim survival. Your HP and stamina are entirely determined by what you eat — a well-fed Viking can have 300+ HP while a starving one dies in one hit. This guide covers every crop, recipe, cooking station, and food strategy for the entire game.

    🌱 Crops & Farming

    Farming Basics

    1. Craft a Cultivator at the Forge (5 Bronze + 5 Core Wood).
    2. Equip the Cultivator and right-click on ground to prepare a cultivated plot.
    3. Plant seeds (right-click with seeds in inventory).
    4. Wait — crops take 1–3 in-game days to grow.
    5. Harvest — pick up mature crops. Some produce new seeds.

    Requirement Details
    Cultivated soil Use cultivator on grass/dirt
    Sunlight Crops need open sky (no roof)

    Crop Progression Strategy

    Stage 1 (Black Forest): 
  - Collect Carrot Seeds from Black Forest
  - Plant 20+ carrots for cooking
  - Replant 15 to maintain seed stock

Stage 2 (Swamp):  
  - Find Turnip Seeds (yellow flower — rare spawn, mark on map!)
  - Plant 20+ turnips
  - Turnip Stew is your best mid-game HP food

Stage 3 (Mountains):
  - Find Onion Seeds in mountain chests
  - Plant 20+ onions
  - Onion Soup is the best stamina food until Plains

Stage 4 (Plains):
  - Raid Fuling villages for Barley + Flax
  - Start Plains farm immediately — Barley/Flax only grow in Plains
  - Build a walled farm with portal access
  - 100+ Barley, 50+ Flax is the target

Stage 5 (Mistlands):
  - Find Jotun Puff + Magecap in Mistlands
  - Build Mistlands farm on flat terrain
  - Jotun Puff for Misthare Supreme (best HP food)
  - Magecap for magic builds

    Seed Management

    Crop One-Time Purchase? Ongoing
    Carrot Find 20 seeds → plant → harvest → replant 3x seeds per carrot Self-sustaining after 1st batch
    Turnip Find 10 seeds → same process Self-sustaining
    Onion Find 10 seeds → same process Self-sustaining
    Barley Harvest from Plains → replant all → get more Self-sustaining (no seeds needed)
    Flax Harvest from Plains → replant all → get more Self-sustaining (no seeds needed)
    Jotun Puff Harvest in Mistlands → replant Self-sustaining
    Magecap Harvest in Mistlands → replant Self-sustaining

    ⚠️ Critical: Never eat your last seeds! Always replant at least 50% of your harvest to maintain supply.

    🍳 Cooking Stations

    Cauldron Progression

    The Cauldron is your primary cooking station. It upgrades to unlock better recipes.

    Station Unlock Condition Upgrade Materials Recipes Unlocked
    Cauldron (L1) Cooked Meat on campfire → craftable 10 Tin Basic recipes: Cooked Meat, Boar Jerky, Deer Stew, Queens Jam
    Spice Rack (L2) Upgrade 5 Dandelion + 3 Carrot + 5 Mushroom + 3 Thistle Turnip Stew, Sausages, Carrot Soup, Onion Soup
    Butcher Table (L3) Upgrade 4 Finewood + 2 Core Wood + 2 Bronze Wolf Skewer, Lox Pie, Serpent Stew, Blood Pudding
    Pots & Pans (L4) Upgrade 5 Iron + 3 Finewood + 4 Leather Scraps Misthare Supreme, Honey Glazed Chicken, Spicy Bread
    Rolling Pin (L5) Upgrade 10 Jotun Puff + 5 Magecap + 5 Black Marble Ashlands-tier recipes

    Other Cooking Stations

    Station Crafting Materials Function
    Campfire 5 Stone + 2 Wood Cook raw meat (basic), base heat
    Cooking Station 10 Wood + 2 Leather Scraps Auto-cook meat (up to 3 pieces)
    Hearth 15 Stone + 3 Iron Indoor fire — supports larger cooking setup
    Stone Oven 20 Stone + 5 Iron Decorative cooking — bakes bread visually
    Fermenter 30 Finewood + 5 Bronze + 10 Resin Brews meads and potions
    Windmill 30 Stone + 20 Wood + 4 Iron Grinds barley into barley flour (for bread)
    Spinning Wheel 20 Finewood + 10 Iron + 5 Leather Spins flax into linen thread

    🐝 Beekeeping & Honey

    Honey is a critical ingredient in meads, cooked foods, and more. You need a steady supply.

    Finding Bees

    1. Look for abandoned structures in the Meadows (stone ruins, small houses).
    2. Inside, look for a Bee Hive hanging from the ceiling — it has a buzzing sound.
    3. Destroy the hive with any weapon (3 hits).
    4. You'll get a Queen Bee (guaranteed drop).
    5. If you destroy a hive and DON'T get a queen, check for more hives nearby.

    Building a Beehive

    Requirement Details
    Recipe 1 Queen Bee + 10 Wood
    Craft at Workbench
    Placement Outdoors only (open sky)
    Spacing At least 2m between hives
    Production 1 Honey per hive per ~20 minutes real time

    Beehive Optimization

    Honey farm layout (10 hives):

    3m     3m     3m
  ┌──┐   ┌──┐   ┌──┐
  │🐝│   │🐝│   │🐝│    ← Row 1
  └──┘   └──┘   └──┘
    3m     3m     3m
  ┌──┐   ┌──┐   ┌──┐
  │🐝│   │🐝│   │🐝│    ← Row 2
  └──┘   └──┘   └──┘
    3m     3m     3m
  ┌──┐   ┌──┐   ┌──┐
  │🐝│   │🐝│   │🐝│    ← Row 3
  └──┘   └──┘   └──┘

Place on a 1m-high post with a roof 3m above.
Bees fly out to the edges — keep clear.

    Honey Usage

    Use Honey per Unit Priority

    Medium Healing Mead 10 High
    Major Healing Mead 10 High

    Stamina Mead 10 High

    Honey target: Maintain a chest of 100+ honey at all times. You burn through it fast with mead production.

    🧪 Fermenter & Mead Basics

    The Fermenter converts mead bases into usable potions. Fermentation takes 2–3 in-game days.

    Fermenter Setup

    Placement:
- Outdoors (or in a room with open roof — fermenter produces smoke)
- Flat ground
- Accessible from all sides (2m gap)

Fermenter base recipe:
10 Honey + [other ingredients] + 1 stack of any wood
→ produces 6 potions after 2–3 in-game days

    All Mead Recipes

    Mead Recipe (in Fermenter) Effect Use Case
    Minor Healing 10 Honey + 5 Raspberry + 2 Blueberry + 1 Dandelion +75 HP instant Early game emergency heal
    Medium Healing 10 Honey + 5 Bloodbag + 2 Raspberry +150 HP instant Mid-game (Swamp+)
    Major Healing 10 Honey + 5 Bloodbag + 2 Jotun Puff +250 HP instant Endgame (Mistlands+)
    Stamina 10 Honey + 10 Cloudberry + 10 Carrot +150 Stamina instant Boss fights, long journeys
    Tasty 10 Honey + 10 Raspberry + 5 Blueberry +300% Stamina regen for 20s Building, escape
    Poison Resist 10 Honey + 5 Coal + 1 Thistle + 1 Neck Tail +75% Poison resist for 10m Mandatory for Swamp
    Frost Resist 10 Honey + 5 Bloodbag + 2 Greydwarf Eye +75% Frost resist for 10m Mandatory for Mountains
    Fire Resist 10 Honey + 5 Coal + 2 Surtling Core +75% Fire resist for 10m Mandatory for Ashlands
    Barley Wine 10 Barley + 10 Cloudberry +30% Stamina for 10m Optional

    Mead Production Priority

    Priority 1 (craft 10+): 
  Minor Healing → Medium Healing → Major Healing
  (scale as you progress)

Priority 2 (craft 5+):
  Poison Resist (before Swamp)
  Stamina (before any boss fight)

Priority 3 (craft 3+):
  Frost Resist (before Mountains)
  Fire Resist (before Ashlands)
  Tasty Mead (for building sessions)

Priority 4 (craft when needed):
  Barley Wine (specialized use)

    Mead Usage Tips

    • Stacking meads: You can have a healing mead + a resist mead active simultaneously.
    • Resist meads last 10 minutes — reapply before exploring a biome, not when you're already taking damage.
    • Healing meads are instant-use. Keep them on your hotbar.
    • Tasty mead is amazing for building (infinite stamina for 20s) but terrible in combat (stamina regen crash afterward).
    • Fermenter efficiency: Each fermenter batch produces 6 potions. Build 2–3 fermenters for bulk production.

    Farm Automation Tips

    While Valheim doesn't have Factorio-level automation, you can streamline:

    Technique How-To Benefit
    Bulk replanting Hold right-click + walk over a row of cultivated soil Fast replanting of 50+ seeds
    Staggered harvest Plant 1/3 of crops every day Continuous supply instead of feast/famine
    Proximity portal Portal directly at your farm Quick harvest from any base
    Multi-level farm Build farm plots on different terrain levels Space efficiency, scenic
    Animal pen Fenced area + portal nearby Quick access to boar/wolf/lox meat
    Bee wall Line the perimeter wall with beehives Space-efficient honey production
