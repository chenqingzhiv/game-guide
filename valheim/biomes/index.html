    🌍 Valheim Biome Guide — Complete 7-Biome Walkthrough

    Last updated: July 2026 | Game version: 0.219.14+

    Valheim's world is divided into seven distinct biomes, each harder than the last. Entering a new biome unprepared means death — often in seconds. This guide covers every biome in progression order, with entry requirements, danger ratings, complete resource tables, recommended gear, base-building tips, and boss associations.

    Quick Biome Overview

    # Biome Danger Level Recommended Gear Boss Key Resource
    1 🌲 Meadows Low Leather / Rags Eikthyr Deer Hide, Flint
    2 🌳 Black Forest Medium Troll Hide / Bronze The Elder Copper, Tin, Surtling Cores
    3 💀 Swamp High Iron / Root (Poison Resist) Bonemass Iron Scraps, Bloodbags
    4 🏔️ Mountains High Silver / Wolf Fur (Frost Resist) Moder Silver, Obsidian
    5 🌾 Plains Very High Padded / Root Chest Yagluth Black Metal, Barley
    6 🌫️ Mistlands Extreme Carapace / Feather Cape The Queen Black Marble, Soft Tissue
    7 🔥 Ashlands Extreme Ashlands-tier (Fire Resist) Fader Flame Metal, Charcoal
    8 🧊 Deep North ??? (1.0) TBA TBA TBA

    🌲 1. Meadows — The Starting Biome

    The Meadows is your spawn biome. Lush green hills, gentle streams, and relatively peaceful wildlife. Meadows is safe enough to learn the basics — hunting, gathering, building — but don't get cocky: Boars and Graydwarves (at night) can kill an unprepared player.

    Terrain & Hazards

    Hazard Description How to Handle
    Boars Aggressive when provoked — charge attack Hunt with bow from range; parry with a shield
    Graydwarf (night) Spawns at night only — moderate damage Stay inside at night or build a campfire ring
    Necks Small lizard-like creatures near water Easy kill with any weapon; drops Neck Tail for cooking
    Bees If you destroy a bee hive, they swarm Don't destroy hives until you have a beehive ready

    Resources

    Resource Use How to Find
    Wood Everything — tools, buildings, fire Chop any tree
    Stone Axes, hammer, basic building Ground pickups or Boulder deposits
    Flint Knives, arrows, sharpening Riverbanks — look for shiny grey rocks
    Deer Hide Leather armor, upgrades Hunt deer with bow
    Leather Scraps Workbench upgrades, tools Kill boars
    Raspberry / Mushroom Early food — 20 HP / 20 Stamina Bushes / ground pickups
    Honey Cooking, mead base Inside abandoned structures (bee hives)
    Core Wood (edge) Better building poles Fallen trees near Black Forest border

    Recommended Gear

    Slot Item Why
    Weapon Crude Bow + Flint Axe Range for deer, melee for boars
    Shield Wood Shield Basic blocking
    Armor Leather Tunic (or rags) Minimal — just avoid hits
    Food Cooked Meat + Raspberry + Mushroom Balanced HP/stamina for learning

    Base Building Tips

    • First base: Build near water (coastline) with access to the Black Forest border.
    • Location: Open meadows, away from Black Forest (graydwarves wander in).
    • Defense: A simple stake wall is enough. Moats are overkill here.
    • Portal hub: Meadows is the ideal location for your main portal hub — central, safe, easy access.

    Boss Association

    Eikthyr — summoned with 2 Deer Trophy + 1 Greydwarf Eye at his stone altar. See the Boss Strategies guide for the fight.

    🌳 2. Black Forest — The Copper Age

    Dense, dark forests with a greenish hue. The Black Forest is your first real challenge — aggressive enemies roam day and night, and the terrain is hilly with deep ravines. This is where you transition from survival to industry.

    ⚠️ Critical Tip: Never travel the Black Forest at night without a rested bonus and good gear. Night spawns double the enemy count.

    Terrain & Hazards

    Hazard Description How to Handle
    Graydwarf Standard enemy — moderate damage Block, counter, 3–4 hits
    Graydwarf Brute Large variant — heavy slam attack Parry timing is critical; roll away if unsure
    Graydwarf Shaman Ranged poison attack — spawns poison clouds Kill first; poison resist helps
    Troll Giant blue humanoid — massive damage Do not melee early on. Use bow + fire arrows + kite
    Burial Chambers Dark dungeons with skeletons and respawning enemies Bring a torch + stagbreaker (AOE through walls)
    Rocky terrain Steep hills, ravines — easy to fall Use pickaxe to terrace paths

    Resources

    Resource Use How to Find
    Copper Ore Bronze (with Tin) Dark brown seam deposits — visible on map as brown patches
    Tin Ore Bronze (with Copper) Near water — riverbanks and coastal cliffs
    Surtling Core Portal, smelter, charcoal kiln Burial Chambers — yellow glowing orbs; also found in Fire Geysers
    Finewood Finewood Bow, furniture Birch and Oak trees — need a bronze axe to chop
    Core Wood Better building poles (tall structures) Pine trees — chop with flint axe or better
    Ancient Seed Summon The Elder Burial Chambers — yellow orbs
    Resin Fire arrows, torches All Black Forest enemies drop some
    Blueberries Queen's Jam, meads Bushes — harvest with any tool
    Troll Hide Troll armor (light, sneaky) Kill Trolls — ~50% drop rate

    Recommended Gear

    Slot Item Why
    Weapon Finewood Bow + Fire Arrows Kite trolls, clear graydwarves safely
    Melee Bronze Sword or Mace Brutes and shamans need melee
    Shield Bronze Buckler Parry window is generous; can parry brutes
    Armor Troll Hide (light) or Bronze (heavy) Troll hide for mobility + sneak; bronze for face-tanking
    Food Cooked Meat + Deer Stew + Queen's Jam Solid HP + stamina pool
    Mead Minor Healing (3+) Emergency burst heal

    Base Building Tips

    • Location: Build near the coast with both copper and tin access.
    • Multiple bases: A mining outpost in the Black Forest with a portal and smelter reduces travel time.
    • Defense: Trolls can destroy wooden walls. Dig a moat or raise earth walls.
    • Portal: Always place a portal at your Black Forest base. You will die here.
    • Smelter location: Build the smelter at your main base (Meadows), not in the Black Forest — moving bronze bars is safer than defending a smelter in hostile territory.

    Boss Association

    The Elder — summoned with 3 Ancient Seeds at his stone platform. Weak to fire. See the Boss Strategies guide.

    💀 3. Swamp — The Iron Grind

    The Swamp is a miserable, rain-soaked hellscape. Poison, leeches, and constantly wet debuff make this the game's first real wall. The water is deep and murky, visibility is poor, and the enemy density is oppressive.

    ⚠️ Mandatory Prep — Do not enter the Swamp without Poison Resist Mead, a blunt weapon, and a hoe (to raise ground paths).

    Terrain & Hazards

    Hazard Description How to Handle
    Poison clouds (gasp) Green mist patches — deal poison damage every tick Avoid or pop poison resist; detect life with Wishbone later
    Draugr Armed undead — bow, sword, or spear variants Parry + blunt weapon; Draugr Elite (starred) is dangerous
    Blob / Oozer Poison AOE on death — explodes into poison cloud Kill at range or back away after killing
    Skeleton Basic undead — weak but numerous Blunt weapon one-shots them
    Leech Water-dweller — poison bite Bow from land; don't wade in
    Abomination Giant tree creature — very high HP, resistant to pierce Axe or fire arrows; kite around indestructible trees
    Wet debuff Permanent rain in Swamp — reduces stamina regen by 15% Always have Rested buff active before exploring
    Deep water Slow movement, leeches, drowning risk Raise ground with hoe to create safe paths

    Resources

    Resource Use How to Find
    Iron Scraps Iron bars (smelted at blast furnace) Sunken Crypts — muddy scrap piles inside (center of rooms)
    Ancient Bark Bonemass summon, Draugr Fang bow Ancient tree pickups (they fall when you chop nearby trees)
    Bloodbag Medium Health Mead, Blood Pudding Leeches drop them
    Entrails Sausages (best swamp food) Draugr and Draugr Elites
    Ooze Poison arrows, Ooze bombs Blobs and Oozers
    Core Wood Building Chop fallen trees
    Withered Bone Summon Bonemass (10 needed) Skeletons in Sunken Crypts
    Chanterelle Cooking ingredient Glowing blue mushroom — ground pickup
    Turnip Turnip Stew (unlocked after boss) Found in Swamp — yellow flowering plant (rare) — mark location!
    Guck Poison arrows, building materials Under water — glowing green deposits (drain water or dive)

    Recommended Gear

    Slot Item Why
    Weapon Iron Mace (blunt) Bonemass is immune to pierce; Draugr shred with blunt
    Backup Huntsman Bow For Abominations and emergency ranged pulls
    Shield Iron Buckler or Banded Shield High block armor for parrying Draugr Elites
    Armor Root Chest (pierce resist) + Iron Legs/Helm Root chest blocks Draugr arrows; iron for overall protection
    Food Sausages + Turnip Stew + Queen's Jam ~220 HP / 160 Stamina — minimum for survival
    Mead Poison Resist (always active), Minor/Medium Health, Stamina Poison resist lasts 10 min — reapply before exploring crypts
    Tool Hoe Raise paths out of deep water — this is non-negotiable

    Sunken Crypt Farming — Best Strategy

    1. Find a crypt — look for stone archway structures in the Swamp.
    2. Enter with a stagbreaker — you can smash Muddy Scrap Piles through walls by hitting the ground beside them.
    3. Use the Hoe — inside crypts, raise ground to drain water for easier movement.
    4. Per-crypt yield — 40–120 Iron Scraps depending on room count.
    5. Portals: Place a portal on top of the crypt (build a ladder on the outside — the crypt is indestructible).
    6. Target: 160 Iron Scraps minimum before leaving the Swamp biome.

    Base Building Tips

    • Do not build in the Swamp for permanent bases. Use tree houses on the indestructible ancient trees.
    • Portal platform: Build a 3x3 platform in an ancient tree. Place portal + workbench + chest.
    • Main base: Return to Meadows or Black Forest for permanent structures.

    Boss Association

    Bonemass — summoned with 10 Withered Bone at his skull altar. Weak to blunt. See the Boss Strategies guide.

    🏔️ 4. Mountains — The Silver Frontier

    Snow-capped peaks with howling winds and sub-zero temperatures. The Mountains biome is cold — without frost resist, you take damage every 5 seconds and eventually freeze to death. Wolves hunt in packs, Drakes patrol the sky, and the terrain is treacherous.

    ⚠️ Mandatory: Frost Resist Mead OR Wolf Fur Chest before your first expedition. Frost resist lasts 10 minutes — carry 3+ doses.

    Terrain & Hazards

    Hazard Description How to Handle
    Freezing temperature Deals damage without frost protection Wolf Fur Cape or Frost Resist Mead
    Wolf Fast, packs of 2–5 — high damage Parry + silver weapon (bonus spirit damage vs undead)
    Wolf (starred) 1-star: 4x HP; 2-star: 10x HP — extremely dangerous Avoid if possible; bow from range; parry + 5–6 hits
    Drake Ranged frost attack from above Bow — they flee after taking ~50 damage
    Stone Golem Very high HP, resistant to pierce and blunt Pickaxe does bonus damage; or use an iron pick
    Frost Caves Underground caves with Cultists and Ulvs Bring a torch; Cultists drop Fenris Hair (light armor crafting)
    Falling Cliff edges, ravines — instant death Build pathways with hoe; carry 10 wood for emergency stairs
    Blizzard Random weather event — zero visibility Find shelter (cave or build a small hut) and wait it out

    Resources

    Resource Use How to Find
    Silver Ore Silver weapons, armor, Frostner Silver veins are underground — use the Wishbone (from Bonemass) to find them
    Obsidian Obsidian arrows (high damage) Black rock deposits on mountain surfaces
    Wolf Pelt Wolf armor, Wolf Fur Cape Wolves drop pelts
    Wolf Fang Wolf Skewer (great food) Wolves drop
    Drake Trophy Decoration, but also the Moder spawn item requirement Shoot down Drakes
    Dragon Tear Artisan Table (unlocked after Moder) From Moder's altar — pickup after killing her
    Onion Seeds Onion soup — best stamina food for mountains Found in Mountain chests (rare)
    Fenris Hair Fenris armor set (light, fast) Cultists in Frost Caves
    Cultist Trophy Fenris armor upgrade Cultists drop — farm in Frost Caves
    Crystal Crystal battleaxe, windows Golems and Frost Cave deposits

    Recommended Gear

    Slot Item Why
    Weapon Draugr Fang (bow) + Frostner (mace) Draugr Fang's poison ticks are excellent against high-HP golems; Frostner slows wolves
    Backup Silver Sword Spirit damage bonus vs undead (bonus damage vs skeletons, draugr)
    Shield Silver Shield High block armor — parry wolf packs
    Armor Wolf Fur Chest + Padded Legs + Silver Helm Frost resist from chest; heavy armor everywhere else
    Food Wolf Skewer + Lox Pie + Blood Pudding ~255 HP / 260 Stamina
    Mead Frost Resist (mandatory), Major Health, Stamina Frost resist refreshes every 10 minutes
    Tool Wishbone (from Bonemass) Absolutely required to find silver — it pulses faster when near silver ore

    Silver Mining — Wishbone Mechanics

    1. Equip the Wishbone in your utility slot.
    2. Walk across the mountain — when the wishbone pulses faster and beeps, you're near silver.
    3. Dig down with the iron pickaxe. Silver veins are large chunks ~1020 units per node.
    4. Mark the location on your map for portal placement.
    5. Yield: Each silver node gives ~90–140 silver ore. You need ~63 for full set + weapon.

    Base Building Tips

    • Mountain outpost: Build a small stone hut near the Moder altar. Use a portal for resupply.
    • Avoid open peaks: Wolves can climb. Build on a sheltered ledge.
    • Bonfires: Multiple bonfires in your mountain base provide warmth (helps counteract freezing).
    • Pathways: Use a hoe + pickaxe to flatten paths between POIs.

    Boss Association

    Moder — summoned with 3 Dragon Tear at a stone altar on the highest peak. Weak to pierce/poison. See the Boss Strategies guide.

    🌾 5. Plains — The Black Metal Frontier

    Golden, open plains under a warm sun — don't let the beauty fool you. The Plains are merciless. Deathsquitos can one-shot you without pierce resistance, Fuling patrols are aggressive, and Lox will trample your base.

    ⚠️ Deathsquito warning: 75 pierce damage per hit. Root Chest gives pierce resist = 75% damage reduction. Wear it.

    Terrain & Hazards

    Hazard Description How to Handle
    Deathsquito Fly — 75 pierce damage (can one-shot through light armor) Block with shield or wear Root Chest; they die in 1 hit
    Fuling Green goblin — standard melee and spear variants Parry + counter; respect starred varieties
    Fuling Berserker Large Fuling — spinning club attack (devastating) Roll away from spin; punish the pause after
    Fuling Shaman Ranged fireball + heal — heals nearby fulings Kill first; bow from max range
    Lox Giant furry beast — AOE ground slam, trample Bow from range (30+ arrows); Lox can destroy stone
    Growth Slime-like puddle — persistent poison AOE Stay out of the green cloud; kill with blunt from range
    Tar Pits Sticky tar + Growth spawns Approach carefully; bring fire arrows
    Mosquito swarms (night) Group deathsquito attacks at night Stay indoors or near campfires at night

    Resources

    Resource Use How to Find
    Black Metal Scrap Blackmetal weapons, tools Fulings drop — need Blast Furnace to smelt
    Flax Linen thread (padded armor, blackmetal) Fuling villages — harvest in fields
    Barley Bread, Lox Pie, Blood Pudding Fuling villages — harvest in fields
    Cloudberry Stamina meads, food Plains bushes — glowing orange
    Needle Needle arrows, Porcupine (mace) Deathsquitos drop needles
    Lox Meat Lox Pie — best Plains food Hunt Lox
    Lox Trophy Decoration ~15% drop from Lox
    Fuling Totem Summon Yagluth Fuling Berserkers and Shamans
    Tar Darkwood building materials Tar pits (dangerous — clear Growth first)

    Recommended Gear

    Slot Item Why
    Weapon Blackmetal Sword or Porcupine High base damage; Porcupine has bonus pierce (great vs fulings)
    Bow Draugr Fang (still top-tier) Poison arrows for Lox hunting
    Shield Blackmetal Shield High block armor for Berserker parries
    Armor Root Chest (pierce resist) + Padded Legs/Helm Root chest is non-negotiable for deathsquito survival
    Food Lox Pie + Bread + Blood Pudding ~210 HP / 265 Stamina
    Mead Major Health, Stamina, Fire Resist (optional) Fire resist helps against Shaman fireballs

    Plains Village Raiding

    1. Scout first — approach from a hill and count fulings, berserkers, shamans.
    2. Mark the village — place a portal 200m away before attacking.
    3. Snipe shamans — kill shamans first (bow from 50m). They prevent the revive mechanic.
    4. Berserkers last — kill berserkers by parrying their 3-hit combo, then landing 4–5 hits.
    5. Harvest — flax and barley are in the fields. Harvest everything. Bring them to safety immediately.
    6. Per village: average 20–40 Black Metal Scrap, 30–50 Flax, 20–30 Barley.

    Base Building Tips

    • Main base: Don't build in the Plains unless you have stone walls and a deep moat.
    • Lox proofing: Lox can destroy stone walls in 3 charges. A 4m wide moat stops them.
    • Fuling raids: After killing Yagluth, fuling raids can happen anywhere. Your Plains outpost needs defenses.
    • Breeding ground: Plains is excellent for Lox breeding (open space) once it's walled off.

    Boss Association

    Yagluth — summoned with 5 Fuling Totem at a giant stone hand altar. See the Boss Strategies guide.

    🌫️ 6. Mistlands — The Magic Frontier

    The Mistlands are Valheim's most visually oppressive biome — a thick, magical fog reduces visibility to 10 meters. The terrain is vertical: soaring rock spires connected by invisible bridges. Without the Wisplight, you're blind.

    ⚠️ Mandatory gear: Wisplight (crafted from a Wisp + Yggdrasil Wood at the Artisan Table). Without it, you can't see more than 5 meters and will fall off cliffs constantly.

    Terrain & Hazards

    Hazard Description How to Handle
    Thick Mist Reduces visibility to 8–10m Wisplight extends vision to 20m; Wisp Torches reveal more
    Vertical terrain Spires, cliffs, invisible bridges Feather Cape (no fall damage) is a game changer here
    Seeker Giant insect — fast, armored, high damage Carapace arrows from range; Mistwalker's spirit damage bypasses armor
    Seeker Soldier Armored variant — can only be damaged from behind or with pickaxe Roll past, hit the unarmored rear
    Tick Small parasite — latches on, drains HP Roll to shake off; kill before it attaches
    Dvergr Neutral dwarves — hostile if you steal from them Don't anger them; their base is a safe zone and can be used for shelter
    Dvergr Rogue Fast, dual-dagger attacks High DPS — block or parry
    Dvergr Mage Magic attacks — AOE frost/lightning Interrupt with bow; kill fast
    Giant Passive giant NPCs — unkillable Leave them alone; they can crush enemies if led to them
    Mistlands rain Magic rain — reduces stamina regen by 30% Rested buff is even more critical here

    Resources

    Resource Use How to Find
    Black Marble High-tier building material Giant skulls and structures — mine with pickaxe
    Soft Tissue Carapace armor, Mistwalker Seekers and Seeker Soldiers drop
    Carapace Carapace armor, carapace arrows, construction Seekers drop

    Navigation Tips

    • Feather Cape makes Mistlands infinitely easier — no fall damage means you can jump off cliffs freely.
    • Wisp Torches — place them along your path to create safe zones with full visibility.
    • Invisible bridges — the rock spires are connected by walkways. Look for the slightly different-colored terrain texture.
    • Dvergr outposts — safe zones. Build a portal inside an unoccupied Dvergr tower.
    • Veteran tip: Build a road system using the hoe's "level ground" function. Flat paths between POIs save hours of vertical climbing.

    Base Building Tips

    • Mistlands base — find a relatively flat area near water (rare but exists). Build with Black Marble walls.
    • Portal network — Mistlands is huge and vertical. Place a portal on every major spire.
    • Safe rooms — build small stone huts every 500m with a campfire (rested buff) and a portal.

    Boss Association

    The Queen — summoned with 9 Seal Fragment at an Infested Citadel. See the Boss Strategies guide.

    🔥 7. Ashlands — The Endgame

    Volcanic wasteland at the southern edge of the world. Lava flows, ash storms, and the game's toughest enemies. Ashlands is the final biome (as of v0.219.14) and demands max-tier gear, all available meads, and absolute focus.

    ⚠️ Mandatory: Fire Resist Mead + full set of Ashlands-tier gear. The ground itself deals fire damage without fire protection. You have been warned.

    Terrain & Hazards

    Hazard Description How to Handle
    Lava flows Rivers of lava — instant death on contact Build bridges with stone/marble; use hoe to raise paths
    Ash storms Reduces visibility, deals fire damage over time Take shelter; Fire Resist Mead helps
    Charred Warrior Swordsman — fast, aggressive Parry + 3-hit combo; watch for spin attack
    Charred Archer Ranged — fire arrows Shield block; close distance fast
    Charred Mage AOE fireballs — devastating at range Bow snipe from 50m+; interrupt casts
    Charred Warlord Boss-level enemy with shield and AOE slam Treat like a mini-boss; parry carefully; backstab if possible
    Fallen Valkyrie Flying — dive-bomb attack Bow while dodging — 10–12 carapace bolts
    Surtling (Ashlands) Lava-dwelling fire spirit Kill from range; drops Charcoal Cores
    Lava blobs Stationary — explode when you approach Don't approach; destroy with arrows from safe distance

    Resources

    Resource Use How to Find
    Flame Metal Ashlands-tier weapons, armor, tools Charred enemies drop; smelted at Blast Furnace
    Charcoal Core Ashlands portals, fuel Surtlings and Charred Mages drop
    Ashwood Fire-resistant building material Charred structures — chop with any axe
    Dyrnwyn Fragment Summon Fader Fortress at Ashlands center

    Recommended Gear

    Slot Item Why
    Weapon Flame Metal Sword or Demolisher (2h hammer) Highest base damage; fire damage bonus with Fader power
    Ranged Spinesnap (Ashlands crossbow) + Carapace Bolts 180+ damage per hit
    Shield Ashlands Shield (Flame Metal) Highest block armor in the game
    Armor Ashlands Carapace Set Fire resist passive — reduces Ashlands terrain damage
    Food Boar Jerky + Spicy Bread + Fish Wrap ~320 HP / 300 Stamina
    Mead Fire Resist (mandatory), Major Healing (10+), Stamina (5+) Fire resist is permanently required in Ashlands
    Utility Bonemass Power (for Fader) +75% resist to all physical damage — life saver

    Fortress Raid Strategy

    1. Scout — find the central Fortress (marked on map after finding Dyrnwyn clue).
    2. Perimeter clean — clear all Charred enemies within 200m.
    3. Entrance — destroy the front gate with a pickaxe or Demolisher.
    4. Room-by-room — Ashlands Fortresses are multi-room dungeons. Clear each room before advancing.
    5. Loot room — the Dyrnwyn Fragment is in the central chamber, guarded by a Warlord.
    6. Portal room — build a portal inside the Fortress after clearing it.

    Base Building Tips

    • Permanent base: Ashlands is not for permanent bases. Build a fortified outpost with Black Marble + Ashwood walls.
    • Fire-proofing: Everything must be stone or black marble. Wood burns instantly here.
    • Portal bunker: Build a fully enclosed marble room for your portal. The room needs a roof — flying enemies attack portals.
    • Bonfires: They help with cold in Mountains. In Ashlands, they add to the heat damage — don't place them.

    Boss Association

    Fader — summoned with 1 Dyrnwyn Fragment + 3 Flame Metal at a peak altar. See the Boss Strategies guide.
