    Enshrouded Weapons Guide

    Enshrouded features 8 weapon classes, each with unique movesets, damage types, and stat scaling. Choosing the right weapon for your build and upgrading it through material tiers is essential for survival. This guide covers every weapon type, the best weapons at each tier, and the complete upgrade system.

    Weapon Classes Overview

    Class Damage Speed Range Best For
    Swords Medium Fast Medium Balanced, all-around combat
    Axes High Slow Short High single-target damage
    Maces High Slow Short Stun-lock, shield builds
    Spears Medium Medium Long Reach, group control
    Bows Medium Fast Very Long Ranged DPS, headshots
    Wands Low Very Fast Long Magic DPS, elemental procs
    Staves Very High Very Slow Long AoE magic, burst damage
    Daggers Low Very Fast Very Short Quick attacks, crit builds

    Damage Type & Elemental System

    Each weapon deals one of these damage types:

    Damage Type Effective Against Weak Against
    Physical (Slash) Flesh enemies, animals Armored enemies (50% reduction)
    Physical (Blunt) Armored enemies, skeletons Flying enemies (75% reduction)
    Physical (Pierce) Flying enemies, lightly armored Heavy armor (40% reduction)
    Fire Undead, ice enemies Fire-resistant enemies (Obsidian zone)
    Ice Wyrms, fire enemies Ice-resistant enemies (Summits)
    Lightning Mechanical, water enemies None (universally effective)
    Shroud All enemies (slight bonus) Shroud-resistant bosses

    Tip: Carry at least 2 weapon types — one physical, one elemental. Swap based on enemy resistances.

    Upgrade System

    All weapons can be upgraded at the Blacksmith or Alchemist (for magic weapons). Each tier significantly increases damage and adds stat bonuses.

    Upgrade Tiers

    Tier Level Range Damage Bonus Stat Bonus Materials Required
    1 (Common) 1–5 +25% None Resin x5 + String x3
    2 (Uncommon) 6–10 +50% +1 to primary stat Metal Scraps x5 + Leather x3
    3 (Rare) 11–15 +75% +2 to primary stat Metal Bars x5 + Infused String x5
    4 (Epic) 16–25 +100% +3 to primary stat + minor bonus Hollow Cores x5 + Palladium x5
    5 (Legendary) 26–30+ +125% +5 to primary stat + major bonus Dragon Scales x5 + Shroud Crystals x5

    Upgrade Priority

    1. Weapon first — Upgrading your weapon gives the largest DPS increase per material spent
    2. Main hand weapon only — Focus materials on one weapon rather than splitting between two
    3. High-damage weapons benefit more — Upgrading a 40 damage weapon to Legendary (+125%) adds 50 damage vs upgrading a 20 damage weapon which only adds 25

    Weapon Tips & Tricks

    1. R1 and R2 are different — Light attacks (R1) are faster but lower damage. Heavy attacks (R2) are slower but stagger enemies.
    2. Charged attacks — Holding the attack button charges it for bonus damage. A fully charged heavy attack on an axe does 3x base damage.
    3. Combo system — Light-light-heavy is the most efficient DPS combo for most melee weapons. Light-heavy is better for quick engagements.
    4. Weapon durability — Each weapon has 500–800 durability. A full repair costs 20% of the crafting materials. Carry a backup weapon.
    5. Parry windows — Swords and shields can parry. The parry window is 0.5 seconds (double the dodge's 0.25s invulnerability window).
    6. Elemental arrows — Craft fire, ice, and poison arrows at the Hunter workbench. Different arrow types do 50% more/less damage based on enemy weakness.
    7. Weapon reach matters — Spears have the longest reach (5m), daggers the shortest (1m). Use weapons appropriate for the enemy's threat level.
    8. Two-handing — Equipping a two-handed weapon removes your off-hand slot but increases damage by 25%.
    9. Sneak attacks — Attacking enemies from behind while crouched deals 3x damage with daggers, 2x with all other weapons.
    10. Status effects — Fire burn (10 damage/tick for 5s), Ice slow (30% slow for 3s), Poison (15 damage/tick for 8s). Procs stack.
